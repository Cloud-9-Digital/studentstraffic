- [x] **Status: Complete** — `/app/mbbs-in-russia-without-neet/page.tsx` created; data in `study-abroad-guides.ts` key `mbbs-in-russia-without-neet`

**Content brief:**

Angle: Definitive, honest answer to the NEET question for Russia. This query has commercial intent from agents who tell students they can bypass NEET by going to Russia. The page should correct this misconception clearly.

Must-have sections:
1. Direct answer upfront: You cannot go to Russia without NEET if you intend to practise medicine in India. Russian universities do not require NEET for admission, but NMC does before you can take NExT/FMGE.
2. What "without NEET" actually means: Russian university admission does not need NEET. The visa does not need it. But you will be blocked from the NMC eligibility certificate (required before departure per NMC rules) and from NExT if you do not have a qualifying NEET score.
3. The NMC Eligibility Certificate: what it is, why it is mandatory before you travel, that NMC checks for a valid NEET-UG score during this process
4. What students who went without NEET are facing: unable to register with NMC, unable to clear NExT, stuck with a degree they cannot use in India
5. Who can go without NEET: students who do not intend to practise in India (e.g., planning to settle abroad)
6. NEET marks required: no cutoff for foreign universities, but you must have attempted and qualified (biology/physics/chemistry 50% aggregate equivalent)
7. FAQ: Can I go to Russia for MBBS and then appear for NEET later? What happens if I start MBBS in Russia without a NEET score?

What to avoid: Do not make this page sound punitive or lecture-y. Many families have already been misled by agents. Write it with empathy, not judgment. Do not give them false hope about workarounds.

## Execution rules

1. **Build Phase 1 tasks before Phase 2.** The quick wins (low KD) establish topical authority that helps the Phase 2 pages rank faster.
2. **Static pages over blog posts when both could work.** Static pages get permanent navigation presence and are prioritised differently by crawlers. Blog posts are for informational/decision content that benefits from a byline and date.
3. **Do not create a page for any keyword under 10 volume** unless it is a conversion-critical query (e.g., `/mbbs-in-russia-without-neet` is worth building despite low volume because it intercepts a harmful misconception that loses leads).
4. **University enrichment tasks (Phase 3) can run in parallel** with Phase 2 page builds — they do not depend on each other.
5. **Internal links are not optional.** Every new page must link to the main pillar (`/mbbs-in-russia`) and at least 2 other existing Russia pages before publishing.
6. **Mark the checkbox when the page is live, crawlable, and not returning a 404.** Do not mark complete if the page is drafted but not deployed.
