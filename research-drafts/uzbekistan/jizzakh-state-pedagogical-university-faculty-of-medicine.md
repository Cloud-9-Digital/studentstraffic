# Jizzakh State Pedagogical University - Faculty of Medicine (Jizzakh, Uzbekistan)

Research date: 2026-07-07
Status: **Publish-ready (conservative/honest draft)** - gate checks pass, but this is a thin/cautious page. See "What was omitted and why" below.

## Key facts (corroborated)

- **Parent university**: Jizzakh State Pedagogical University, named after Abdulla Qodiriy (Kadyri). Founded 8 August 1974 (government resolution + Ministry of Public Education order No. 256), following the formation of Jizzakh Region on 30 December 1973. Named after writer Abdulla Qodiriy in 1989. Granted full **university** status in 2022 by presidential initiative (previously "institute"). Celebrated its 50th anniversary in 2024.
- **City**: Jizzakh, administrative center of Jizzakh Region, eastern Uzbekistan. Sharply continental climate (Jan lows ~ -4°C, July highs ~35°C). Smaller/regional city compared to Tashkent, Samarkand, Bukhara.
- **Faculty of Medicine**: Officially established **14 December 2020** (Order No. 654, Ministry of Higher and Secondary Specialized Education). First intake of 66 students began **2021-22 academic year** in two directions: General Medicine and Pediatrics. Roots trace to a Department of Life Safety (1979) and Department of Fundamentals of Medical Knowledge (1983).
- **Current scale**: ~245 students (140 General Medicine / 105 Pediatrics), ~65 professors/instructors, 1 department (Preclinical Sciences) at last official update (2023 data on the faculty history page). Dean as of the source snapshot: Laziz G'oziyev (PhD).
- **Programs**: Bachelor's-level "General Medicine" (Davolash ishi, code 60910200) and "Pediatrics" (Pediatriya ishi, code 60910300). Official pages describing these are in Uzbek.
- **Clinical training partners** (named on official site): Republican Specialized Cardiology Center, Regional Multidisciplinary Hospital, Emergency Medical Care Center, Regional Perinatal Center, an Oncology/Dermatology/Children's Hospital grouping. Also 10 classrooms allocated at Abu Ali ibn Sino Jizzakh Public Health Technical College.
- **International cooperation**: University-wide International Cooperation Department exists, handling international-student admission/registration/advising, MoUs, Erasmus+/DAAD/KOICA participation, and visa-related paperwork (invitation and admission-confirmation letters). No medical-faculty-specific international program page was found.
- **Dormitory**: University-wide Student Residence exists (renovated), described (via secondary source, e.g. Edugain Overseas) as multi-person suites with shared bathrooms/kitchens/laundry. Not medical-faculty-specific.

## What was OMITTED and why

1. **Tuition/fees** - Omitted entirely. No official tuition figure for international/contract-basis medical students was found on the official site. Third-party consultancy sites (studyinuzbekistan.com, ensureeducation.com, pacificeducation.in, etc.) quote generic ranges ($1,500-$3,500/year) for the university broadly, not specific to the Medical Faculty, so no figure is trustworthy enough to publish. Per rules, we do not fabricate a fee or use a placeholder 0.

2. **NMC / FMGE / regulator recognition** - Not asserted. Several MBBS-consultancy/marketing sites (ensureeducation.com, studyinuzbekistan.com) claim this institution is "approved for Indian students" or among "top 10 medical colleges," but:
   - These claims do not appear corroborated on the official JDPU site.
   - Cross-checked against a third-party list of NMC-approved Uzbekistan medical colleges (Leverage Edu), Jizzakh does not appear; the commonly cited NMC-recognized institutions are Tashkent Medical Academy, Samarkand State Medical University, Bukhara State Medical Institute, Andijan State Medical Institute, Tashkent State Dental Institute, etc.
   - Given the faculty is very new (est. Dec 2020) this is plausible either way, but since it is NOT corroborated, no NMC/FMGE claim is made in the draft. The FAQ explicitly tells Indian students to verify NMC recognition and NExT/FMGE eligibility directly with NMC before enrolling.

3. **English medium of instruction** - Not asserted for the Medical Faculty specifically. The university's site is trilingual (Uzbek/Russian/English) and one secondary source claims "all courses are available in English, Russian, and Uzbek," but the official Medical Faculty program pages themselves are published in Uzbek (program names "Davolash ishi", "Pediatriya ishi", no English-medium curriculum page found). The draft flags this as a caveat and tells students to confirm directly.

4. **Indian food / Indian student community** - No official or credible secondary source found describing this specifically for Jizzakh (unlike Tashkent/Samarkand/Bukhara, which have established Indian student populations). The `indianFoodSupport` field is grounded honestly in this absence rather than invented.

5. **courseSlug choice** - The gate's fixed taxonomy only allows `mbbs | medical-pg | bsc-nursing | pharmacy | bds`. "General Medicine" (Davolash ishi) is the structural/regional equivalent of an MBBS-track undergraduate medical degree in the former-Soviet system, so `mbbs` was used, with `durationYears: 6` (standard for this track region-wide) and medium noted as Uzbek/Russian with an explicit "verify before applying" caveat baked into the `medium` field itself.
