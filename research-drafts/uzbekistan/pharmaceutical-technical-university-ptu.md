# Research Document: Pharmaceutical Technical University (PTU), Tashkent, Uzbekistan

Status: PUBLISH-READY draft (gate checks pass). Country slug: `uzbekistan`. Draft slug: `pharmaceutical-technical-university-ptu`.

## Key facts (corroborated)

- **Founding**: Created as the educational core of the Uzbek government's "Tashkent Pharma Park" innovative scientific-production pharmaceutical cluster, established by Presidential Decree No. PP-4574 dated 28 January 2020 (Uzbek President Shavkat Mirziyoyev). Confirmed independently by both a UzDaily.uz news article (6 April 2022) and the Embassy of Uzbekistan in India's own site.
- **Phase I construction / financing**: A Cabinet of Ministers resolution dated 4 April 2022 approved implementation of Phase I (university buildings, teaching/research labs, preclinical research centre, pharmacopoeial centre, library, sports/recreation complex, hostel), financed in significant part by an approximately US$83.7 million, 40-year loan (10-year grace period) from South Korea's Eximbank Economic Development Cooperation Fund (EDCF). Total project cost estimated at ~US$98.6 million per the feasibility study cited by UzDaily.
- **Type**: State/public university, under the Agency for the Development of the Pharmaceutical Industry (itself under Uzbekistan's Ministry of Health), per WebSearch summaries of gov.uz and the agency's own portal.
- **First teaching year**: 2024/25 -- confirmed directly by De Montfort University's (DMU) own official news article (published 13 Feb 2025) covering PTU's formal opening, attended by President Mirziyoyev and DMU staff Duncan Hepworth (Partnership Development Manager) and Dr Neil Hart (Associate Director of Transnational Education).
- **International academic partners**: 
  - **De Montfort University (DMU, Leicester, UK)** -- involved with the PTU project since ~2019 (per DMU's own account); validates a pharmacy foundation ("year zero") programme progressing to **MPharmacy** and **BSc (Hons) Cosmetic Science**.
  - **University of Sunderland (UK)** -- validates progression to **BSc Biomedical Science**.
  - Also named (per DMU article): **Yonsei University** (South Korea) and **Purdue University** (USA) as partners in the broader project, though their specific degree contributions were not detailed in sources reviewed.
- **First cohort details**: DMU's article names Anna An, a 17-year-old from Tashkent, as one of the first students on DMU's foundation pharmacy programme at PTU, and as PTU's first-ever elected Student Union president. Two PTU lecturers (Nargiza Eshbekova, Kamola Narzikulova) travelled to DMU in Leicester in 2021/22 to complete an MSci in Pharmaceutical Quality by Design specifically to prepare to teach at PTU -- evidence of pre-launch faculty development investment.
- **Location**: Tashkent Pharma Park cluster, Zangiata / Yangihayot district area, on Tashkent's outer ring road (Tashkent Halqa Avtomobil Yuli), near Zangiata-1/Zangiata-2 landmarks -- per a Golden Pages Uzbekistan business-directory listing. The cluster is described as a ~20-hectare site (per DMU's article).
- **Programme focus**: NOT a medical school -- PTU is a pharmacy/pharmaceutical-sciences/cosmetic-science institution. No MBBS, BDS, or nursing program exists here.

## What was OMITTED and why

1. **Tuition/fees** -- No official or independently reliable source published a specific PTU tuition figure. Generic ranges surfaced in search summaries referred to unrelated Uzbek medical universities, not PTU. Per the hard rule against fabricating fees, this was omitted entirely (honest `feeNotes` explains this and directs students to ask admissions).
2. **Hostel: confirmed operational status** -- A hostel/dormitory is named in the 2022 Cabinet resolution's Phase I construction scope, but no source confirms it is complete and in operation for the current cohort. Draft frames this honestly as "planned, not confirmed operational" rather than describing it as an available amenity.
3. **NMC (India) relevance** -- Not applicable; PTU is a pharmacy university, not a medical school, so NMC (which governs MBBS/medical recognition) is irrelevant here. The draft correctly identifies **PCI (Pharmacy Council of India)** as the potentially relevant regulator instead, and finds no confirmed PCI approval for PTU (unsurprising given the university is one cohort old), and explicitly tells students to verify with PCI directly.
4. **Detailed campus amenities (labs, library, sports facilities, dining)** -- Official PTU sites (ptu.uz, pharmatechuni.uz) are JavaScript single-page apps that returned only an empty ~3.6KB HTML shell to automated curl fetches (multiple user agents tried, both `/` and `/about` paths, on ptu.uz and the alternate pharmatechuni.uz domain) -- no rendered body text was retrievable via static fetch. Facility descriptions in the draft are therefore limited to what corroborated third-party/government sources describe (e.g., the Phase I construction plan's facility list), rather than invented specifics.
5. **Indian-student-specific services** (mess, association, etc.) -- No source described any PTU-specific Indian food or Indian-student community services; the draft is explicit that this is unverified/likely nonexistent given the university's newness, and redirects to Tashkent's general Indian restaurant scene instead.
