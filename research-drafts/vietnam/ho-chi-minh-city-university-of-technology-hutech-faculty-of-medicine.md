# Ho Chi Minh City University of Technology (HUTECH) - Faculty of Medicine

**Status: HOLD - not publish-ready.** Country: Vietnam. City: Ho Chi Minh City.
Research date: 2026-07-07.

## Why this is on hold

HUTECH's General Medicine ("Y khoa") program is **brand new** and, based on
exhaustive multi-source research, has **no confirmed English-medium
instruction and no confirmed international/Indian-student admissions
pathway**. Publishing an India-audience MBBS-abroad page for it now would
require fabricating facts (student life, clinical exposure for foreign
students, hostel arrangements, Indian food access, safety framing, NMC/NEET/
FMGE recognition angle) that could not be corroborated from any official or
independent source. Per project sourcing rules (exhaustive-then-omit, never
fabricate), this is held as a local research record only.

## Key verified facts

- **University**: Ho Chi Minh City University of Technology (HUTECH /
  Truong Dai hoc Cong nghe TP.HCM). Private university, founded 26 April 1995
  under Prime Ministerial Decision No. 235/TTg. Multiple campuses in Ho Chi
  Minh City (main campus at 475A Dien Bien Phu, plus Ung Van Khiem and Thu
  Duc campuses per secondary sourcing). ISO 9001:2015 and AUN-QA
  institutional/programme quality frameworks; QS 4-Star overall rating
  (November 2022); QS World University Rankings 2027 band 1401+.
- **Medicine program approval**: The General Medicine bachelor's/professional
  program (code 7720101) was approved by Vietnam's Ministry of Education and
  Training via **Decision No. 1372/QD-BGDDT, dated 26 May 2026** — i.e., only
  weeks before this research was conducted.
- **School of Medicine and Health Sciences**: HUTECH announced establishment
  of an internal "Truong Y va Khoa hoc Suc khoe" (School of Medicine and
  Health Sciences) on **2 June 2026**, grouping General Medicine, Pharmacy,
  Nursing, and Medical Laboratory Technology under one school.
- **Program structure**: 6 years, 210 credits, awarding a "Bac si Y khoa"
  (General Medicine Doctor) degree. Curriculum covers standard pre-clinical
  subjects (Anatomy, Physiology, Biochemistry, Microbiology, Immunology,
  Pharmacology) and clinical subjects (Internal Medicine, Surgery, Obstetrics,
  Pediatrics, Medical Imaging, Anesthesiology, Family Medicine, Psychiatry,
  Rehabilitation), oriented toward newer teaching trends (clinical
  simulation, medical data/AI, e-portfolios, simulated electronic patient
  records) per search-summarized official/news content.
- **2026 first-cohort enrollment**: Only **50 students**. Admission methods
  are entirely Vietnam-domestic: 2026 national high-school graduation exam
  scores, high-school transcript/academic-record method, V-SAT scores, and
  VNU-HCM (Vietnam National University - Ho Chi Minh City) aptitude-test
  scores — plus, as an alternate equivalency route within HUTECH's *general*
  (not medicine-specific, not India-specific) admissions system, international
  qualifications such as SAT/ACT/A-Level/IB.
- **Scholarship**: A 30% full-course-tuition scholarship was announced for
  the first cohort of Medicine (and other Health Sciences programs), with a
  no-tuition-increase commitment through the course, per official/news
  sourcing. Indicative Vietnamese-domestic fee figures were seen in Vietnamese
  ("Nganh Y khoa: 28 trieu dong/dot, tron khoa 24 dot") but these apply to the
  domestic admission track and were NOT treated as a reliable, India-relevant,
  currency-converted tuition figure for this draft — hence tuition was
  omitted entirely rather than estimated.
- **Language of instruction**: Every official HUTECH page found describing
  this program (homepage, `khoa-hoc-suc-khoe` faculty landing page, the
  Nursing/Medical-Technology faculty page, and the admissions announcement,
  as summarized via search) is **Vietnamese-language only**. No `/en/`
  English version of the Medicine program pages was found. HUTECH does
  separately operate an "International Institute" (Vien Dao tao Quoc te)
  offering English-medium bachelor's programs in business/technology fields
  with foreign university partners (Lincoln University US, Open University
  Malaysia, CY Cergy Paris University, Tongmyong University Korea, Kanazawa
  Institute of Technology Japan, etc.) — but this international track is
  **entirely separate from, and does not extend to, the new Medicine
  program**.
