# Research record: "Alytaus Kolegija (Alytus University of Applied Sciences) – General Practice Nursing"

Status: **NOT PUBLISHED (ok=false)** — held as a local draft only. See reason below.

## Why this could not be honestly published as assigned

The assigned topic asks for a page about "Alytaus Kolegija (Alytus University of Applied Sciences)"
offering a "General Practice Nursing" programme in Alytus, framed for an India audience. Multi-source
research shows this premise no longer matches reality:

1. **Alytaus Kolegija no longer exists as an independent university.** On 1 July 2024 it was
   reorganised into the **Alytus Faculty of Kauno Kolegija / Higher Education Institution**
   (Kaunas University of Applied Sciences) as part of a Lithuanian government state-HEI
   consolidation programme. Official confirmation: the institution's own news announcement
   (see Source 3) and the Lithuanian state education registry AIKOS, which lists "Alytus Faculty
   of Kauno kolegija / Higher Education Institution" as the current legal entity, parent institution
   "Kauno kolegija / Higher Education Institution", registration date 2024-07-11 (Source 6).

2. **The nursing programme actually taught in Alytus is Lithuanian-medium, not English.**
   The Alytus Faculty's own programme catalogue lists "Bendrosios praktikos slauga" (General
   Practice Nursing) under Health Sciences (Source 4), and its dedicated programme page only
   shows a Lithuanian-language study plan with no English admissions track (Source 5). The AIKOS
   registry confirms the Alytus Faculty's studying language is **"lithuanian"** only (Source 6).

3. **The English-medium "General Practice Nursing" Bachelor's programme international/Indian
   applicants would actually enrol in is centrally delivered in Kaunas, not Alytus.** Both the
   institution's own English program page (Source 1) and its official application portal (Source 2)
   state explicitly: "Study location: Lithuania, Kaunas", "Delivered at: Faculty of Medicine",
   tuition €3,100/year, application fee €120, IELTS ≥5.5/TOEFL ≥75/PTE ≥59/CEFR ≥B2 required,
   entrance interview + competence test mandatory. There is no separate English-medium nursing
   intake based in Alytus.

Given this, a page titled/framed as "Alytaus Kolegija – General Practice Nursing" for Indian
students would either (a) misrepresent a Lithuanian-medium, EU/local-facing programme as an
English-medium international offering, or (b) describe a Kaunas-based programme under a
misleading "Alytus" institutional identity — both would be inaccurate/non-corroborated framing,
which the sourcing rules prohibit (never fabricate, never misrepresent). Several third-party
aggregator sites (Study.eu, Standyou, beyondthestates, TopUniversities, Mastersportal) still list
"Alytaus Kolegija" as a live standalone university with English programs — but these appear to be
stale listings that predate the July 2024 merger and are contradicted by the institution's own
current official pages and the Lithuanian government registry, which are the higher-authority,
more current sources.

## What was NOT fabricated / omitted

- No tuition, fee, hostel, or admission facts were invented for an "Alytus-based English nursing
  program" because no such program was corroborated to exist.
- No India-specific regulator framing (INC/NMC equivalence advice) was written, since there is no
  verified English-medium Alytus offering to frame it around.
- No narrative content (campus life, hostel, clinical exposure, Indian food, safety, student
  support) was invented for the Alytus campus in the context of this nursing program, since doing
  so would misrepresent which city/campus the student would actually study in.
