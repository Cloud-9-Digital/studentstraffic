## Identity

- **Full name**: UMCH - University Medicine Campus Hamburg, officially "UMFST-UMCH" (Universitatsmedizin Neumarkt a. M. Campus Hamburg / University Medicine of Targu Mures Campus Hamburg)
- **Relationship**: Privately operated German branch campus of the Romanian **public** university **George Emil Palade University of Medicine, Pharmacy, Science and Technology of Targu Mures (UMFST)**, operated in Germany by CPE Europe GmbH.
- **Location**: Albert-Einstein-Ring 11-15, 22761 Hamburg, Germany - in Hamburg-Bahrenfeld's emerging "Science City" research/university district, next to the University of Hamburg and the DESY physics research centre.
- **Parent university founded**: 1945 (per official UMCH facts-and-figures section and independently corroborated by Wikipedia's article on the George Emil Palade University).
- **Hamburg campus teaching since**: October 2019 (per the Rector's welcome message on the official University page).
- **Type**: Private (branch campus operation), backed by a public Romanian university's accredited degree programme.

## Programs

### MBBS / Human Medicine (Integrated MD) - courseSlug: `mbbs`
- Duration: 6 years / 12 semesters / 360 ECTS
- Medium: English for pre-clinical years 1-2; German/English mix in clinical teaching hospitals years 3-6; free university German course toward C1 by graduation
- Entry: High school/secondary education; English tested at interview
- Intake: September (Fall); 2026/27 cycle deadline stated as 31 July 2026, studies commence 21 Sept 2026
- Official tuition (Non-EU/Indian applicants): **EUR 17,400/semester = EUR 34,800/year** (tuition-fees page), plus one-time EUR 4,500 enrollment fee and EUR 595 application fee. The DreamApply course listing bundles these into an approximate EUR 39,300 first-year headline figure.
- EU/EEA/UK/Swiss applicants: EUR 14,900/semester (lower rate)
- Optional transfer to parent campus in Targu Mures, Romania from year 3: reduces fees to EUR 3,000/year for years 3-6
- No official INR conversion published - omitted per sourcing rules; students told to check current FX rate.

## Accreditation / Recognition (handled cautiously)

- Parent university (UMFST) accredited by Romania's ARACIS (an ENQA member).
- Germany's anabin portal lists the parent university at "H+" (highest status), meaning the degree is recognised as a university qualification in Germany.
- **Important nuance found and preserved honestly**: the official accreditation page states the **English-language Human Medicine programme itself has only reached the first stage ("Provisional Authorization") of accreditation**, awarded by IAAR (recognised by WFME) in 2023. The **second/full accreditation stage is only reached two years after the first cohort of this English programme graduates.** This is a material caveat and is surfaced explicitly in `thingsToConsider` and one FAQ answer rather than glossed over.
- Parent university listed in the World Directory of Medical Schools (WDOMS), FAIMER School ID F0000226 - a prerequisite for USMLE eligibility (US/Canada pathway).
- ISO 9001:2015 certified (TUV Austria).
- **NMC/NEET/NExT/FMGE**: No claim of current NMC recognition status is made for this specific institution/programme. The draft explicitly tells Indian students to verify NEET-qualification requirements and current NMC/NExT status themselves, noting NExT has replaced FMGE for graduates from 2025 onward (per general web-search corroboration, not an official UMCH claim).

## Clinical Training

- From year 3: 30+ named teaching hospitals across Germany (e.g., Elisabeth-Krankenhaus Essen, Helios Klinikum Hildesheim, Sana Klinikum Hameln-Pyrmont, Klinikum Luedenscheid, St. Vincenz Hospital Limburg) + 100+ affiliated teaching practices (general medicine, ophthalmology, dermatology).
- International rotation option: hospitals in Spain (Hospital del Vinalopo, Torrejon University Hospital, Union Murciana de Hospitales y Clinicas) - local-language ability required there.
- Clinical-phase load: ~8 weeks in year 3, rising to 12-16 weeks/year in years 4-6. Students choose preferred teaching hospital from year 2.

## Housing (no university hostel)

UMCH does **not** operate its own dormitories. It curates private partner student-housing providers near campus:
- Felixx: from EUR 635/month
- Urban Living (Ottensen): from EUR 729/month
- THE FIZZ (Altona / Hammerbrook): from EUR 1,063-1,099/month
- HVNS (Hammerbrook / Altona): from EUR 1,299-1,349/month
- Yugo (Wandsbek): from EUR 889/month
- Plus flat-share portal (WG-Gesucht) and international housing platform (HousingAnywhere), and nearby short-stay hotels for arrival.

This is genuinely different from a typical "MBBS abroad" university hostel model and is represented honestly as such (not fabricated as a hostel).

## City Context: Hamburg / Bahrenfeld

- Germany's second-largest city, major port/business hub on the Elbe.
- Campus sits in **Science City Hamburg-Bahrenfeld**, a ~125-hectare research/university district being built around DESY (German Electron Synchrotron) and University of Hamburg science faculties - independently corroborated via Hamburg city and DESY/University of Hamburg sources (not UMCH-authored), used to ground the `cityProfile` narrative field in real, sourced city facts rather than generic filler.
- Large international population; established public transport (S-Bahn Holstenstrasse near some student housing).

## Indian Student Community / Food

- Official UMCH student-life page confirms a dedicated **South Asian Student Society (SASS)** club and published video testimonials from Indian students ("Suhani from India", "Suhani Indian Community").
- No dedicated Indian mess/dining hall is described in official materials - represented honestly as unconfirmed rather than invented; students are told to confirm grocery/restaurant access in their specific housing district with SASS/admissions.

## What Was Deliberately OMITTED (and why)

1. **INR-converted tuition figures** - no official FX date/rate published by the university; only EUR figures are used, with an instruction to check the current rate.
2. **Definitive "NMC recognized" claim** - the accreditation page itself shows the English programme is only at stage 1 of accreditation; asserting NMC recognition would be dishonest. Framed instead as a to-do for the student, with the accreditation-stage caveat made explicit.
3. **Hostel/dormitory framing** - since UMCH has no university-run hostel, the housing section is written around its actual model (private partner housing) rather than forcing a "hostel" narrative.
4. **Any tuition figure for the EU/Romania-transfer track beyond what's officially published** - included only the EUR 14,900 EU/semester and EUR 3,000/year (post-transfer to Romania) figures exactly as published, no extrapolation.
5. **Exact current class size, faculty ratio, or hostel-partner pricing beyond what the official page states** - these can change; the draft notes so and defers to admissions for current confirmation.
