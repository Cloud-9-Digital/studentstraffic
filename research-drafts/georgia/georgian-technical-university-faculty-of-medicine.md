# Research record: Georgian Technical University (Faculty of Medicine) — Tbilisi, Georgia

**Status: HELD / NOT PUBLISHED (ok = false)**

## Summary of finding

The research task named the subject "Georgian Technical University (Faculty of Medicine)," Tbilisi, on
the premise (echoed across many third-party study-abroad agent/aggregator sites) that Georgian Technical
University (GTU) has a "Faculty of Medicine" offering an internationally-facing medical degree.

After checking GTU's own official sources (an official institutional "Facts & Figures" leaflet, GTU's live
official website apply/admissions pages, and GTU's own "Structural Units / Faculties" page), **this could
not be corroborated**. GTU's own materials consistently describe it as a public **engineering/technical
university** with 11–12 faculties covering civil/mechanical/power engineering, informatics, architecture,
design, business, law, mining/geology, and agriculture — **with no Faculty of Medicine and no clinical
medical degree program (MBBS/MD/BDS/Pharmacy/Nursing) in its official English-language program list.**

The one health-adjacent program GTU does offer is **Biomedical Engineering** (Bachelor's/Master's/PhD,
ABET-accredited), housed under the **Faculty of Informatics and Control Systems** — this is an
**engineering** degree (medical instrumentation, biomaterials, medical imaging, artificial implants), not a
clinical qualification, and has no plausible mapping to the required courseSlugs
`{mbbs, medical-pg, bsc-nursing, pharmacy, bds}`.

Per the project's hard rules (never fabricate; exhaustive-then-omit; only assert NMC/PCI/INC relevance if
corroborated), this entity cannot be published as a medical/health-sciences university page for
India-outbound degree-seekers.

## Key facts about Georgian Technical University (for context — not medical-specific)

- Founded 1922 as a polytechnic faculty of Tbilisi State University; became an independent institute (Georgian
  Polytechnical Institute) in 1928; granted university status and renamed Georgian Technical University in 1990.
- Public university, Tbilisi, Georgia. Administrative building: 77 Kostava Str., 0171, Tbilisi.
- Official "Facts & Figures" leaflet reports 12 faculties (Civil Engineering; Power Engineering and
  Telecommunications; Informatics and Control Systems; Transportation and Mechanical Engineering; Mining and
  Geology; Chemical Technology and Metallurgy; Architecture, Urban Planning and Design; International Design
  School; Business-Technologies; Engineering Economics, Media Technology and Social Sciences; Law and
  International Relations) — **no Faculty of Medicine**.
- GTU's current live official faculties page (Georgian-language "Structural Units") lists the same set of
  non-medical faculties; the word "medicine" does not appear anywhere on that page (checked programmatically).
- GTU's official "List of HE Programmes in Foreign Languages" (English-medium Bachelor/Master/PhD degrees)
  includes: Design (B, M), Biomedical Engineering (B, M, PhD — ABET-accredited), Mechanical Engineering (B),
  Business Administration (M), Water Engineering (M, PhD), Viticulture and Enology (M), Agronomy (M), plus
  dual-degree programs with San Diego State University (Electrical Engineering, Computer Engineering, Chemistry
  and Biochemistry, Civil Engineering, Construction Engineering). **No medical degree appears on this list.**
- GTU is a member of the European University Association (EUA, since 2001), part of the Bologna Process (since
  2005), and has extensive EU-funded research/exchange participation (Tempus, CBHE, Horizon/FP7, Erasmus+).

## The "Faculty of Medicine" claim — why it does not hold up

A recurring, near-identical sentence appears across many third-party sites (georgiatostudy.com,
admissionex.com, alchetron.com, and others), all stating GTU "has 14 faculties ... including Faculty of
Medicine." This phrasing traces to what looks like a single shared (likely Wikipedia-derived) boilerplate
description that has been copy-pasted across SEO/agent sites without independent verification.

Contradicting evidence:
1. GTU's own official "Facts & Figures" leaflet names 12 faculties by title and Medicine is not among them.
2. GTU's own official English-language program catalogue (same leaflet) lists zero medical degrees.
3. GTU's live official website (both English apply pages and the Georgian "Structural Units" page) shows the
   same non-medical faculty list, with zero occurrences of "medicine."
4. Even the agent site (georgiatostudy.com) that repeats the "Faculty of Medicine" line, in its own detailed
   Bachelor/Master/PhD tuition tables for GTU, lists only Civil Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, Biomedical
   Engineering, Computer Science, Architecture, and Design at Bachelor's level — no MBBS/MD/BDS/Pharmacy/Nursing
   degree — directly contradicting its own "Faculty of Medicine" boilerplate line.
5. Some agent sites additionally run a generic marketing blurb ("GTU announces admission for Spring 2025 intake
   for Medical Doctor, Dentistry, and Computer Science Programs") with no corroborating official program page,
   curriculum, or tuition figures — this reads as reused agent copy, and may reflect confusion with a genuinely
   medical Georgian institution (e.g., Tbilisi State Medical University, which does run a well-documented
   USMD/Emory-partnered MD program) rather than a verified GTU fact.
