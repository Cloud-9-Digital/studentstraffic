# Georgian-American Nursing College (GANC) - Research Record

Status: **HELD, NOT PUBLISHED** (ok=false). See reasoning below.

- Full name: LLC "Georgian-American Nursing College" (GANC)
- Founded: December 2016, Tbilisi, Georgia
- Legal form: LLC (private), self-governing vocational education institution
- Address: 65 Ketevan Tsamebuli Ave., Tbilisi, Georgia (postal code 0144)
- Contact: Hotline 032 274 74 08 / +995 32 274 74 08; email info@ganc.ge (site) / gancollege@gmail.com (official catalogue PDF)
- Official website: http://ganc.ge/en/
- Director: Maia Jashi, MD
- Background: co-founded by the US-based corporation "Partners for International Development," established 2003 with support from Emory University and USAID, linking Atlanta (Emory University, medical/healthcare systems of Emory and Grady) with Tbilisi's healthcare system.
- Facilities: describes computer-controlled training mannequins/simulation equipment; has clinical-placement (memorandum) agreements with leading Georgian clinics.

## Why this is NOT a fit for the standard MBBS/BSc-Nursing publish pipeline

1. **It is a vocational (VET) college, not a university.** GANC repeatedly self-describes as delivering "vocational education" / "professional education" (Georgian: პროფესიული განათლება), regulated under Georgia's Law on Vocational Education, not the higher-education law/NCEQE university-accreditation track used for MBBS/BSc programs at actual universities.

2. **The confirmed, currently-registered program is a modular "Practical Nurse" vocational program**, not a Bachelor of Science in Nursing:
   - Registration number: 09101-p
   - Qualification level: 5th level per the European Qualifications Framework (EQF)
   - Awarded qualification: "Practical Nurse, 5th-level professional qualification"
   - Volume: 149 credits (179 credits for students who need supplementary Georgian-language instruction)
   - Duration: approx. 27 study months (32 months for students needing extra Georgian language study)
   - Admission precondition: complete general (secondary) education only
   - Legal basis: Georgia's Law "On Vocational Education," National Qualifications Framework, and the Practical Nurse occupational standard

3. **Instruction is in Georgian, not English.** The official program catalogue lists Georgian language modules (A2 level: 15 credits; B1 level: 15 credits) as compulsory general modules alongside English-as-a-foreign-language and IT/communication modules — a pattern typical of Georgian-medium VET programs for the domestic labour market, not an English-taught international program.

4. **A second, higher-credit nursing program exists only conditionally.** The same catalogue describes an "Associate Degree in Nursing / Fifth Level Vocational Qualification in Nursing" (registration 09109-p, 180/210 credits, effective from Feb 29 2020) but frames it as a program GANC "will additionally implement... in case the program is added to the college" — i.e., authorized in the regulatory framework but not confirmed as an actively enrolling, currently-running program. This could not be verified as live without direct confirmation from GANC.

5. **No evidence of an international/Indian-student pathway.** No official page, third-party education-agent site, or search result showed evidence of English-medium delivery, international-student admissions, or foreign professional-council recognition (e.g., India's Indian Nursing Council / INC) for GANC. Multiple targeted web searches turned up only generic/templated agent-portal content (e.g., admissionoffice.ge) that was not specific to GANC and appeared boilerplate (identical "How to Study Dentistry" filler text repeated regardless of query topic) - explicitly excluded as unreliable per sourcing rules.

6. **Career outcomes described are domestic.** The catalogue's "career opportunities" section lists Georgian healthcare institutions/settings (hospitals, ambulatories, diagnostic centers, home care, orphanages/elderly homes, the Ministry of Health, etc.) — consistent with a program designed to feed the Georgian domestic labour market, not international licensure pathways.

## Recommendation

Hold GANC as a local draft only. Do not publish under the current MBBS/BSc-Nursing India-audience template. If the business wants a page for Georgian vocational/practical-nurse training aimed at a different audience (e.g., domestic or non-degree-seeking students), that would require a different content template and an honest reframing — not a retrofit of this one. If GANC later confirms (directly, in writing) that it runs an active English-medium Associate Degree in Nursing open to international students with a clear recognition pathway, this research should be revisited with that specific confirmation as a new primary source.
