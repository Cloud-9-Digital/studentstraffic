# NOSM University (Northern Ontario School of Medicine University) — Research Record

**Status: NOT PUBLISHABLE (ok=false).** This university has no program that is open to Indian/international
students as a study-abroad admission pathway. Publishing a page for this school under our India-audience
"study abroad" framing would misrepresent an institution that explicitly excludes this audience from its only
degree-entry programs. This record documents what was found and why the page is being held rather than published.

## Key facts (corroborated)

- **Name:** NOSM University (Northern Ontario School of Medicine University); legal French name Université EMNO.
- **Type:** Public, independent Ontario university (became a standalone degree-granting university on April 1, 2022;
  Wikipedia; Sudbury.com).
- **Founded:** Founded in 2002 as a joint medical school of Laurentian University (Sudbury) and Lakehead University
  (Thunder Bay); welcomed its first MD students in September 2005; became Canada's newest fully independent
  university in 2022 following the 2021 Laurentian University financial crisis (Wikipedia; CBC News, "NOSM
  University marks 20 years").
- **Campuses:** Two co-equal main campuses — one on the Lakehead University campus in Thunder Bay, Ontario, and one
  on the Laurentian University campus in Sudbury (Greater Sudbury), Ontario (NOSM.ca, "Thunder Bay and Sudbury
  Campuses").
- **Model:** Distributed, community-engaged learning (DCEL) model; partnerships with 500+ organizations across 90+
  Northern Ontario communities and 1,800+ clinical/human/medical-science faculty (NOSM.ca "About NOSM University").
- **Official website:** https://www.nosm.ca/

## Programs offered and eligibility (this is the reason for non-publication)

1. **MD Program (UME — undergraduate medical education), Doctor of Medicine**
   - Applications go through OMSAS (Ontario Universities' Application Centre), the same portal used by all six
     Ontario medical schools.
   - **Eligibility is restricted to Canadian citizens or permanent residents (landed immigrants) of Canada** — this
     is stated directly on NOSM's own "Minimum Requirements" admissions page and corroborated independently by
     third-party admissions-consulting sources (BeMo, AcceptedTogether).
   - MCAT is not required/considered; GPA minimum 3.0/4.0; selection uses OMSAS + CASPer-style non-cognitive
     assessment and Northern/rural/Indigenous-background-weighted criteria (BeMo).
   - **Conclusion: closed to Indian applicants applying from India.** No international admission stream exists.

2. **Physician Assistant (PA) Program — Bachelor of Science, Physician Assistant (BScPA)**
   - Delivered as a consortium program between the University of Toronto, NOSM University, and The Michener
     Institute of Education at UHN; the degree is conferred by the University of Toronto.
   - Independently corroborated eligibility (CanadianPA.ca and general Canadian PA-program admissions guidance):
     **Canadian citizens or permanent residents of Canada only.** Applicants on temporary/study visas are explicitly
     described as not eligible.
   - **Conclusion: also closed to Indian applicants.**

3. **Postgraduate Medical Education (residency, CaRMS-matched specialty and family medicine training)**
   - NOSM does host International Medical Graduates (IMGs) in postgraduate residency positions, with dedicated IMG
     Learner/Faculty Resource Leads, a peer-mentorship program, and an Assessment Verification Period (AVP) for
     IMGs entering via CaRMS or the Advanced Level Postgraduate Training Program (NOSM.ca, "IMG Support and
     Resources").
   - However, this is **not a "study in Canada from India" undergraduate/entry pathway** in the sense our site
     covers (MBBS/BDS/Pharmacy/Nursing degree programs for Indian students). It is a post-MD residency-matching
     process for physicians who already hold a foreign medical degree, requires an existing MD, CaRMS eligibility,
     and separate Canadian immigration/licensing steps (Medical Council of Canada AVP/NAC pathway) well beyond the
     scope of a prospective-student program listing, and is not equivalent to any of our seed `courseSlug` values
     (mbbs, medical-pg meaning postgraduate specialty study is also generally match/residency-based and not a
     direct-enrolment "medical-pg" degree-purchase pathway of the kind covered elsewhere on the site).
   - No structured, direct-entry "programs" array item can be honestly built from this without misrepresenting a
     competitive physician-matching process as a normal admissions program.

## Why this fails the publish gate honestly

The seed/publish gate requires `structuredFacts.programs` to contain at least one program with an explicit
`courseSlug` in `{mbbs, medical-pg, bsc-nursing, pharmacy, bds}`, plus a full non-weak narrative (summary,
clinicalExposure, hostelOverview, indianFoodSupport, studentSupport, etc.) framed for Indian applicants. Every
verifiable NOSM University program (MD, PA) explicitly and exclusively requires Canadian citizenship or permanent
residency, corroborated by NOSM's own official admissions pages and independent third-party sources (BeMo,
AcceptedTogether, CanadianPA.ca). There is no honest way to write hostel/food/safety/clinical-exposure narrative
"for Indian students choosing to study medicine here" when Indian students categorically cannot enrol. Fabricating
an eligible-sounding program or omitting the citizenship restriction would violate the omit-not-fabricate and
non-editorial-honesty rules.

## Recommendation

Do not publish a NOSM University page in the India-facing study-abroad catalogue. If NOSM's policies change in the
future (e.g., an international admissions stream is introduced) or if the site's scope later expands to cover
IMG/residency-matching content for already-qualified doctors, this record can be revisited. No JSON draft file is
being written since no gate-passing program-eligible content exists to populate `structuredFacts.programs`;
fabricating one would violate the no-fabrication rule.
