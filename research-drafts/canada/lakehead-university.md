# Lakehead University (School of Nursing, BScN) - Research Document

Country: Canada | City: Thunder Bay, Ontario | Status: ok=true (publish-ready draft)

## Key facts (corroborated)

- **Type**: Public university.
- **Established**: 1965 (Lakehead University Act, royal assent June 22, 1965). Institutional roots trace back to Lakehead Technical Institute, established June 4, 1946, and Lakehead College of Arts, Science and Technology (1957). [Wikipedia]
- **Location**: Thunder Bay, Ontario, on the northern shore of Lake Superior; second campus in Orillia, Ontario. [Wikipedia; Lakehead official]
- **Enrolment**: Historical Wikipedia figures cite ~6,729 students (2017 factsheet); Lakehead's own 2022 press release cites ~9,700 full-time-equivalent students across both campuses more recently. Both figures are cited in the draft as an approximate range since they come from different years/methodologies.
- **Thunder Bay city**: population ~108,843 (2021 census), ~113,834 (2024 estimate per World Population Review), CMA ~123,258. Largest city in Northwestern Ontario; historic grain-shipping/transportation hub, now diversifying into health care/education "knowledge economy."

## Nursing program

- **Bachelor of Science in Nursing (BScN) - Four-Year Program**. Relaunched as a standalone program for its first cohort in fall 2023 (after Lakehead's School of Nursing, which has taught nursing since 1965, redesigned delivery). Curriculum fully accredited by the Canadian Association of Schools of Nursing (CASN); School of Nursing received a 7-year CASN accreditation (per Lakehead's 2021 news release).
- Clinical practice built into every year (Year 1 long-term care; Year 2 medical-surgical; Year 3 specialties - maternal-infant, child-youth, mental health/addictions, older adult; Year 4 three six-week placements, at least one in the Thunder Bay region).
- Primary clinical hospital: **Thunder Bay Regional Health Sciences Centre** (~395 acute-care beds, serves all of Northwestern Ontario, one of Canada's busiest ERs; supports 1,200+ student placements/year across 25+ disciplines including nursing).
- Graduates are eligible to write **NCLEX-RN** and register with the **College of Nurses of Ontario (CNO)** - this is the Canadian licensing route; distinct from India's Indian Nursing Council (INC). Told students to verify INC recognition separately if planning to practice in India eventually.
- **"Early Finisher" option**: qualifying Year 4 students can start their final Acute Care Preceptorship 6 weeks early (summer term) and finish requirements by mid-February.
- **September intake only** - Nursing is explicitly excluded from Lakehead's limited January undergraduate intake.

## Admission requirements (international / India-specific)

- General: currently reside outside Canada, not a Canadian citizen; secondary-school graduation equivalent; minimum 70% overall (Canadian-equivalent) average.
- Nursing-specific prerequisites: minimum 60% in English, Biology, Chemistry, and Math; competitive admission average of at least 80%.
- India-specific accepted credentials (per Lakehead's "Admission Requirements by Country" page): State Higher Secondary Certificate (State Board), CBSE India/Delhi Senior School Certificate, or ICSE Indian School Certificate. In-progress applicants submit X-level scores, XI final marks, and mid-year/predicted XII results.
- **English proficiency (Nursing-specific, higher than general bar)**: IELTS Academic overall 7.0 (component minimums roughly Writing 7 / Speaking 7 / Reading 6.5 / Listening 6.5), or TOEFL iBT 103 (Writing 28 / Speaking 28 / Reading 24 / Listening 24). General-program minimum is only IELTS 6.5 / TOEFL 80, so Nursing is notably higher.
- May require WES/ICAP credential evaluation at Lakehead's discretion.

## Fees - what was confirmed vs. omitted

- **CONFIRMED and included**: application fee (CAD 135), document evaluation fee (CAD 75 for post-secondary completers), UHIP international health insurance cost (CAD 948 for a 12-month Sept-2026 start), general international undergraduate tuition band from the public 2025-2026 fee schedule (~CAD 29,800-30,300/year across nearly all listed Thunder Bay Honours programs), and ancillary fee ranges (~CAD 1,100-1,500/year).
- **OMITTED (exhausted, not fabricated)**: an exact BScN-specific tuition figure. I paged through all 3 pages of Lakehead's public "Undergraduate Fees List 2025-2026" table (https://www.lakeheadu.ca/students/finances/student-fees/fees/undergraduate-fees/2025-2026, plus ?page=1 and ?page=2) and the standalone four-year BScN/Nursing program was not listed as its own named row or grouped under a "Nursing" program tag in that table (only the Indigenous Nursing Entry Program (INEP) appeared, grouped under an "Arts" fee band, which is a different program). The public interactive Cost Estimator on Lakehead's site is JS-driven and returns "N/A"/"$0.00" placeholders when scraped statically via curl, so an exact number could not be pulled that way either. Rather than reuse a same-band estimate as if it were confirmed Nursing tuition, the draft's feeNotes are explicit that the general band is a cross-program reference point, not a confirmed Nursing figure, and directs students to Lakehead's official Cost Estimator (filtered to Nursing) for a current number.

## City / context facts used to ground otherwise-thin university-specific narrative

- **Crime severity / safety**: Statistics Canada's official 2024 Crime Severity Index data (https://www150.statcan.gc.ca/n1/daily-quotidien/250722/t004a-eng.htm) shows Thunder Bay with the highest violent-crime severity index of the 42 Canadian CMAs measured (more than double the national average) and 6th-highest overall (combined) CSI nationally. Independently corroborated by TBNewsWatch ("Thunder Bay tops violent crime chart in 2024") and CBC News ("Thunder Bay, Ont., once again reports the highest homicide rate in Canada"). This is treated as an important, honestly-stated caveat throughout (cityProfile, safetyOverview, thingsToConsider, and one FAQ), consistent with project standards on honest caveats.
- **Indian/South Asian community**: Vedic Cultural Centre (ISKCON-affiliated Hindu temple, official site, weekly vegetarian Sunday Feast programs) and Thunder Bay Sikh Society's Gurdwara on Spring Street (independently reported by Chronicle Journal and TBNewsWatch, serves free langar to all faiths). No independent source was found confirming specific Indian restaurants/grocery stores in Thunder Bay, so the draft does not claim specifics there beyond the temple/gurdwara facts and honestly notes the community is likely smaller than in major metros.
