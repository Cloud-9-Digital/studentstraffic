# Research record: Université de Montréal — Faculté de médecine (Montreal, Canada)

Status: **NOT PUBLISHABLE for an India-audience MBBS/medical-abroad page.** Held as a local draft only.
Researched: 2026-07-07.

## Why this cannot be published for the target audience

Université de Montréal's Faculté de médecine offers the **Doctorat de 1er cycle en médecine** (undergraduate-entry
MD, 4 years, 1st-cycle French curriculum). Its own official admission-conditions page states the candidate must
satisfy, among other things:

> "avoir la citoyenneté canadienne ou le statut de résident permanent ... à la date limite fixée pour le dépôt de
> la demande d'admission"
> ("hold Canadian citizenship or permanent-resident status ... by the application deadline")
> — https://admission.umontreal.ca/programmes/doctorat-de-1er-cycle-en-medecine/ (fetched 2026-07-07)

The Faculty's own International FAQ page confirms this in plain language, in answer to "I am not a Canadian
citizen (nor a landed immigrant). What are my chances of admission to a Faculty of Medicine program?":

> "Pour les programmes de 1er cycle et plus particulièrement celui du doctorat en médecine, les places réservées
> aux étudiants internationaux sont limitées. En revanche, les programmes de formation à la recherche de 2e et 3e
> cycle offrent beaucoup plus de souplesse quant à l'admission des étudiants étrangers."
> ("For 1st-cycle programs, and particularly the MD, seats reserved for international students are limited. In
> contrast, 2nd/3rd-cycle RESEARCH training programs offer much more flexibility for admitting international
> students.")
> — https://medecine.umontreal.ca/international/foire-aux-questions/ (fetched 2026-07-07)

Independent corroboration (BeMo Academic Consulting) puts a number on this: roughly 95% of MD seats are reserved
for Quebec residents, ~2-3 seats/year go to Maritime/New Brunswick applicants, and only about **2 seats per year**
go to genuinely international, visa-holding applicants, with an international admit rate around 5.3% versus 13%
for Quebec residents. Applicants must also be fluent in French, since UdeM's MD program is entirely French-medium.
— https://bemoacademicconsulting.com/blog/university-of-montreal-medical-school (fetched 2026-07-07)

Because this site's medical-university content targets Indian students seeking a realistic MBBS/medical-study-abroad
route, and the Faculty of Medicine's flagship undergraduate MD program is both (a) legally near-closed to
international applicants (citizenship/PR gate, ~2 seats/year) and (b) delivered entirely in French with a new
C1-level proficiency bar, there is no honest way to write "bestFitFor," a NEET/NMC/FMGE regulator framing, program
admission guidance, fee guidance, or a "why choose this school" narrative for a general Indian applicant audience.
Any such content would be fabricated or misleading. Per the hard rule (never fabricate, honest caveats, omit
rather than invent), this university is being held back rather than force-fit into the publish gate.

## The "DIM" re-entry route was also checked, and does not solve the problem

The Faculty also runs a small re-entry pathway for **Diplômés Internationaux en Médecine (DIM)** — foreign-trained
doctors seeking to (re)join the MD program to requalify in Quebec. This is capped at roughly **2-4 seats per year**
across the whole "contingent international" allocation set by the Quebec government / Comité interfacultaire
d'admission en médecine, and critically **requires Quebec-resident status** (via a Certificat de sélection du
Québec / CAQ process) — it is not a general open door for Indian medical graduates or aspirants, and it re-enters
students into the same French-medium undergraduate-style MD structure rather than offering an
internationally-portable postgraduate/residency credential.
Sources:
- https://medecine.umontreal.ca/international/foire-aux-questions/
- https://www.usherbrooke.ca/etudes-medecine/programmes-detudes/doctorat-en-medecine/admission/contingent-quebecois-categorie-des-diplomes-internationaux-en-medecine
  (independent regional corroboration of the same Quebec-wide DIM/contingent mechanics)

## Graduate research programs (M.Sc./Ph.D.) exist but do not map to this site's taxonomy

The Faculty of Medicine's own FAQ says graduate **research** programs (Master's/PhD in biomedical sciences, etc.)
are much more open to international admission than the 1st-cycle MD. However, these are research degrees (thesis-based
M.Sc./Ph.D.), not a clinical MBBS-equivalent or postgraduate clinical-specialty (residency) pathway. They do not fit
any of this site's allowed courseSlugs (`mbbs`, `medical-pg`, `bsc-nursing`, `pharmacy`, `bds`) — `medical-pg` on
this site specifically means postgraduate **clinical** medical programs (MD/MS specialty training), not thesis
research degrees — so no program entry was created for this route either.

## The same pattern holds across UdeM's other professional health degrees (checked for completeness)

Since the research task was scoped to "Faculté de médecine" specifically, BDS/Pharmacy/Nursing programs sit in
separate UdeM faculties and are out of scope for this university profile. But to sanity-check whether this is a
one-off restriction or a structural pattern, the equivalent professional doctoral programs in UdeM's other health
faculties were spot-checked:

- **Doctorat de 1er cycle en médecine dentaire (D.M.D., Faculté de médecine dentaire):** official admission page
  states outright **"Non offert aux candidats étrangers avec un permis d'études"** ("Not offered to foreign
  candidates holding a study permit") and requires Canadian citizenship/PR at the deadline.
  — https://admission.umontreal.ca/programmes/doctorat-de-1er-cycle-en-medecine-dentaire/
  The Faculty of Dentistry's own International Students page separately states that international students at
  the graduate level are normally directed toward the M.Sc./Ph.D. in oral/biomedical sciences, since other
  programs require authorization to practise dentistry in Quebec.
  — https://medecinedentaire.umontreal.ca/etudes/etudiants-internationaux/
- **Doctorat de 1er cycle en pharmacie (Faculté de pharmacie):** official admission page carries the **identical**
  "Non offert aux candidats étrangers avec un permis d'études" restriction plus a Canadian citizenship/PR
  requirement.
  — https://admission.umontreal.ca/programmes/doctorat-de-1er-cycle-en-pharmacie/
- By contrast, the **Baccalauréat en sciences infirmières** (Faculté des sciences infirmières, a separate faculty)
  did *not* show this "Non offert" banner in the same admission-page template, and UdeM maintains a dedicated
  "Formalités d'immigration" page for international nursing students — suggesting nursing may follow a different,
  more open pattern. This is noted only for context/completeness; it is out of scope here since nursing is a
  separate faculty from "Faculté de médecine," the assigned research target.

This cross-check confirms the MD/DMD/Pharmacy closure is a **structural, university-wide pattern** for UdeM's
professional doctoral programs in the health-exclusive-practice professions (medicine, dentistry, pharmacy) —
not an isolated fact about this one program — which strengthens confidence in the finding rather than undermining
it.

## Key verified facts (for the record)

- **Institution:** Université de Montréal (UdeM), a large public French-language research university, founded in
  1878 as a branch campus of Université Laval (Université Laval à Montréal), granted a papal charter in 1919 and
  a provincial charter in 1920, made fully independent/secular in 1967. Thirteen faculties, including two
  affiliated schools (Polytechnique Montréal, HEC Montréal). Confirmed on Wikipedia; consistent with UdeM's own
  institutional history pages.
  Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Universit%C3%A9_de_Montr%C3%A9al
- **Faculté de médecine:** Offers the Doctorat de 1er cycle en médecine (MD), 4 years full-time, day courses,
  mandatory clinical placements (stages), accredited by the Comité d'agrément des facultés de médecine du Canada
  (CAFMC). Limited intake capacity ("Capacité d'accueil limitée").
  Source: https://admission.umontreal.ca/programmes/doctorat-de-1er-cycle-en-medecine/
- **Eligibility (the disqualifying fact):** Canadian citizenship or permanent-resident status required by the
  application deadline for standard 1st-year entry; a parallel DIM/international-medical-graduate re-entry
  pathway requires Quebec-resident status and is capped at a handful of seats/year, set by provincial/interfaculty
  quota, not by UdeM alone.
  Source: https://admission.umontreal.ca/programmes/doctorat-de-1er-cycle-en-medecine/ ;
  https://medecine.umontreal.ca/international/foire-aux-questions/
- **Language of instruction:** French only. From Winter 2026, UdeM applies a revised French-proficiency
  requirement (CECRL level C1 on accepted tests/diplomas such as TEF, TCF, DALF C1/C2, or UdeM's own French-school
  courses) for any applicant whose prior studies were not entirely in French.
  Source: https://admission.umontreal.ca/programmes/doctorat-de-1er-cycle-en-medecine/ (in-page French-requirement
  widget)
- **International MD admit numbers (independent reporting):** ~95% of seats reserved for Quebec residents; ~2-3
  seats/year for Maritime-region applicants; ~2 seats/year for genuinely international (visa-holding) applicants;
  international admit rate ~5.3% vs. ~13% for Quebec residents (2018/19 cohort cited).
  Source: https://bemoacademicconsulting.com/blog/university-of-montreal-medical-school

  indianFoodSupport, safetyOverview, studentSupport) — left empty. Writing city/lifestyle/hostel/food narrative

  international/Indian applicants (citizenship/PR gate, ~2 seats/year, French-medium).
- **`bestFitFor` and `faq`** — left empty for the same reason; any FAQ answers implying an accessible admissions
  path would be misleading.
- **Tuition/fees** — not researched in depth, since the eligibility exclusion makes fee detail moot for this
  site's use case (same approach as the University of Calgary and Simon Fraser University holds already on file).
- **Indian-regulator framing (NMC/NEET/FMGE/PCI/INC)** — not asserted at all, since there is no admissions path to
  frame in the first place.

## Recommendation

Do not publish. This matches the established pattern for other Canadian medical schools already researched and
held (University of Calgary - Cumming School of Medicine; Simon Fraser University - Stephens Family School of
Medicine): Canadian MD programs are, as a rule, gated to citizens/permanent residents (occasionally + Convention
refugees), with only token international quotas where any exist at all, making them structurally unsuitable for
this site's India-outbound MBBS-abroad product without misleading readers. If the site ever wants a
note-only/informational (non-conversion) page type — e.g., "Canadian medical schools and why most are closed to
Indian MBBS applicants" — that would need separate product sign-off; it is out of scope for the standard
university-profile template used here.
