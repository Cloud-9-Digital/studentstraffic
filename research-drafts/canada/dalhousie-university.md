## Key facts (corroborated)

- **Name:** Dalhousie University
- **City:** Halifax, Nova Scotia, Canada
- **Type:** Public research university
- **Established:** 1818 (as Dalhousie College, non-sectarian, founded by Lieutenant Governor George Ramsay, 9th Earl of Dalhousie; became a degree-granting university in 1863)
- **Official website:** https://www.dal.ca/
- **Scale:** ~21,000 students; 2,800+ residence spaces across Halifax + Truro (Bible Hill/Agricultural) campuses, 11 residences, students from 47 countries
- **Ranking context (per Dalhousie's own India-facing page):** 12th overall in Canada, Top 15 research-intensive university in Canada

Sources: dal.ca homepage, dal.ca/about/our-story/history.html, Wikipedia, The Canadian Encyclopedia.

## Program-by-program international-access research (India-outbound audience)

### DDS (Doctor of Dental Surgery) -- INCLUDED (courseSlug: bds)
- **This is the one program confirmed open to a defined international-applicant quota.**
- Official DDS Admission Fact Sheet (Sept 2025 PDF, cdn.dal.ca) states the most recent published admission profile: ~500 applications, 44 accepted, split as **26 Canadian seats and 18 international seats**.
- 4-year program; requires ~60 university credit hours (about 3 years of prior post-secondary, science-heavy coursework) completed before entry -- NOT a direct-from-Class-12/high-school route.
- Requires minimum A- average (3.7/4.3) in final 60 credit hours and in higher science courses (biochemistry, microbiology, human physiology).
- Requires the Canadian/American Dental Aptitude Test (DAT), NOT NEET -- minimum component scores of 17 on PAT/SNS/RCT; most successful applicants score 20+.
- Application via AADSAS or the Dalhousie portal, deadline October 1.
- Applicants who studied outside Canada/US must submit a course-by-course World Education Services (WES) evaluation (document-by-document not accepted).
- English-proficiency test required if first language is not English.
- **Tuition (confirmed, official 2026-2027 fee schedule PDF):** International DDS tuition = **CAD 62,751.74/year** (all 4 years), versus CAD 31,351.80/year for domestic/Canadian-seat students. Plus mandatory per-term International Student Society Fee and mandatory basic international health insurance (opt-out available with proof of comparable coverage).
- Licensure: graduates become eligible for National Dental Examining Board of Canada (NDEB) certification (Written Exam + OSCE/Virtual OSCE) -- the standard Canadian dental licensing pathway. NOT an Indian degree/recognition claim; students told to verify Indian Dental Council/regulator recognition independently.
- Sources: cdn.dal.ca DDS Admission Fact Sheet PDF (official), dal.ca/study/programs/graduate-professional/dentistry-dds.html (official), cdn.dal.ca 2026-2027 fee schedule PDF (official), ndeb-bned.ca (official licensing body).

This DDS finding is notably **stronger/more specific** than the comparable University of British Columbia DMD research done previously for this site (UBC's DMD profile lacked a published international-seat count or headline tuition figure; Dalhousie's DDS profile has both, directly from official sources).

## City / context facts (Halifax)

- Capital of Nova Scotia, largest city in Atlantic Canada; metro population ~440,000 (2021 census, per Wikipedia/StatsCan-linked sourcing).
- Independent news source (CBC, "Nova Scotia's growing Asian population getting taste of home at new grocery stores") confirms a fast-growing Asian/South Asian population and new Indian/Asian specialty grocery stores opening in the Halifax area, plus a temple serving the community.
- Established South Asian grocery businesses found: South Asian Mart (Dartmouth), New Indian Bazaar (Bayers Lake, described elsewhere as Nova Scotia's largest Indian grocery store), Indian Groceries on Robie Street (established 1990), Shahi Groceries (Clayton Park).
- Dalhousie's own official India-facing recruitment page (dal.ca/dal/india.html) states directly: Indian students in Halifax "will find: Cultural festivals and community events, Indian grocery stores and restaurants, Student societies and peer networks, Supportive campus environment" -- and names a dedicated Student Recruitment Manager for India (Gaurav Chhabra).
- Safety: Statistics Canada's federal "Safe Cities" profile series publishes detailed crime/safety indicators for the Halifax CMA; general secondary sources describe Halifax as comparatively safer/more affordable than Canada's largest metros with a declining crime trend over the preceding decade. No unverifiable "safest city" superlative is asserted.
- Climate: Atlantic Canada winters (cold, snowy) -- flagged as a significant adjustment from India in the content (honest caveat).
