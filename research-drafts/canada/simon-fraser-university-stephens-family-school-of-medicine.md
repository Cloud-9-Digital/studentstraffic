# Research record: Simon Fraser University — Stephens Family School of Medicine (Surrey, BC, Canada)

Status: **NOT PUBLISHABLE for an India-audience MBBS/medical-abroad page.** Held as a local draft only.
Researched: 2026-07-07.

## Why this cannot be published for the target audience

The MD program's own admissions page states, in the "Citizenship" section of the "General" eligibility
requirements, verbatim:

> "Applicants for the MD program must be Canadian citizens or permanent residents. Proof of citizenship or
> permanent residency must be submitted with your application by the application deadline ... Please note that
> our program is unable to accept applications from international applicants."
> — https://www.sfu.ca/medicine/admissions/before-you-apply/general.html (fetched 2026-07-07)

This is a categorical, single-source-but-unambiguous exclusion straight from the official admissions page (not
an inference). Because the site's medical-university content targets Indian students seeking MBBS/medical study
abroad, and this program is legally closed to international/non-resident applicants, there is no honest way to
write "bestFitFor", a NEET/NMC/FMGE framing, program admission guidance, fee guidance, or a "why choose this
school" narrative for an Indian applicant audience. Any such content would be fabricated or misleading. Per the
hard rule (never fabricate, honest caveats, omit rather than invent), this university is being held back rather
than force-fit into the publish gate.

This is a corroborating, not single-source, finding — the school's own general SFU news coverage and local press
describe the same program as new, BC/Canada-focused, primary-care-oriented, with a small inaugural domestic
cohort — consistent with (not contradicting) the citizenship restriction found on the official admissions page.

## Key verified facts (for the record)

- **Institution:** Simon Fraser University (SFU) — the medical school unit is the "SFU Stephens Family School of
  Medicine." SFU itself is a long-established Canadian public research university (founded 1965); the School of
  Medicine is brand new.
- **Location:** Interim home at SFU Surrey (Central City Tower, 13450-102 Avenue, Surrey, BC, Canada V3T 0A3);
  permanent campus planned for 2030 as part of the "Centre Block" development next to Surrey Central SkyTrain
  station, adjacent to SFU Surrey.
  Source: https://www.sfu.ca/medicine/about.html ; https://www.sfu.ca/medicine/about/our-campus.html
- **Program:** MD (Doctor of Medicine), 3-year curriculum built around three sequential year-long courses
  (MEDS 410, MEDS 420, MEDS 430), with early and continuous clinical learning starting in year one.
  Source: https://www.sfu.ca/medicine/programs/md-program/introduction.html ;
  https://www.sfu.ca/medicine/programs/md-program/curriculum.html
- **First cohort:** Reporting is inconsistent between sources on exact class size/start — SFU's own news page and
  press coverage describe an inaugural class beginning studies in August 2026, with class-size figures varying
  between sources (48 in some reporting, a target of 64 by a later cohort in 2027 in others). Given the
  discrepancy, no single class-size figure should be asserted without further corroboration.
  Sources: https://www.sfu.ca/sfunews/stories/2026/06/historic--40m-gift-powers-sfu-s-new-school-of-medicine.html ;
  https://globalnews.ca/news/11892380/historic-40-million-donated-sfu-medical-school/ ;
  https://surreynowleader.com/2026/06/05/philanthropist-couple-donates-40m-to-surreys-sfu-school-of-medicine/
- **Naming/funding:** Named for the Stephens family (founders of Nature's Path, Love Crunch, Que Pasa Mexican
  Foods), who donated CAD 40 million — SFU's largest single gift on record.
  Sources: https://globalnews.ca/news/11892380/historic-40-million-donated-sfu-medical-school/ ;
  https://voiceonline.com/stephens-family-donates-40-million-to-simon-fraser-university-school-of-medicine/
- **Eligibility (the disqualifying fact):** Canadian citizens or permanent residents only; a separate Indigenous
  admissions stream exists for qualified First Nations/Métis/Inuit applicants from across Canada; a BC-residency
  requirement / BC Medical Services Plan enrolment is also referenced for standard applicants. International
  applicants are explicitly not accepted.
  Source: https://www.sfu.ca/medicine/admissions/before-you-apply/general.html
- **Degree requirement to apply:** a completed 4-year undergraduate bachelor's degree (or equivalent) in any
  discipline, at least 90 completed units at time of application, degree completed by June 30 of the admission
  year. (Confirms this is a graduate-entry, North-American-style MD — not a school-leaver MBBS-equivalent path —
  reinforcing that it doesn't map onto the "MBBS abroad for Indian students" product even ignoring citizenship.)
  Source: https://www.sfu.ca/medicine/admissions/before-you-apply/general.html

## What was omitted, and why

- Everything: no admissions/fee/hostel/clinical-exposure/Indian-food/safety narrative for prospective Indian
  MBBS-seeking students was written, because the entire premise (an Indian student being admissible) is false
  per the official source. Writing "bestFitFor," a NEET/NMC/FMGE regulator framing, or "why choose" content would
  require fabricating an eligibility path that does not exist.
- Program `structuredFacts.programs` entry: not created. There is no courseSlug in
  {mbbs, medical-pg, bsc-nursing, pharmacy, bds} that honestly represents a domestic Canadian graduate-entry MD
  program closed to international applicants.
- Tuition/fees: not researched further once the citizenship exclusion was confirmed, since it is moot for this
  site's use case.

## Recommendation

Do not publish. If the site ever wants a note-only/informational (non-conversion) mention of this school (e.g.,
"schools in Canada NOT open to international students"), that would need separate product sign-off — it is out
of scope for the standard university-profile template used here.
