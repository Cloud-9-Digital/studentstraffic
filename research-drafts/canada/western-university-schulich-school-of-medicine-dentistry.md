# Research record: Western University — Schulich School of Medicine & Dentistry

Status: **HELD — not published, gate not met (ok=false)**
Country: Canada · City: London, Ontario (secondary campus: Windsor, Ontario)
Researched: 2026-07-07

## Why this draft is held

This platform's university pages are structured around a fixed set of India/NEET-audience
course slugs: `mbbs`, `medical-pg`, `bsc-nursing`, `pharmacy`, `bds`. Each published program
must have an honest, currently-open pathway that an Indian student could realistically apply to
under one of those slugs, with an explicit official program URL.

After multi-source research (official Schulich admissions pages, official postgraduate medical
education pages, and independent secondary sources), **no such pathway exists at Schulich** for
any of the platform's course categories. Details below. Per the hard "exhaustive-then-omit,
never fabricate" rule, this draft is held rather than published with an invented or misleading
program.

## Key facts (corroborated)

- **Institution**: Western University, home to the Schulich School of Medicine & Dentistry.
- **Founded**: 1881 (medical school); dental school (Schulich Dentistry) formed in 1964.
- **Type**: Public research university (Ontario, Canada).
- **Campuses**: Main teaching campus in London, Ontario; a second MD-program teaching site in
  Windsor, Ontario (same curriculum delivered at both).
- **Official website**: https://www.schulich.uwo.ca/index.html

Sources: [Schulich "Who We Are"](https://www.schulich.uwo.ca/about/who_we_are/) and
[Schulich School of Medicine and Dentistry — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Schulich_School_of_Medicine_and_Dentistry)
(cross-checked; both agree on founding year and campus structure).

## Program-by-program findings

### MD (maps to `mbbs` course slug in our catalog)

- The standard MD Program requires the applicant to be **a Canadian citizen or permanent
  resident at the time of application**. This is the default admission stream and admits
  ~171 students/year, all from Canada.
- There is a separate **"International Pathway"** with **only three (3) supernumerary seats
  per year, restricted to the Windsor Campus only**. Eligibility for this pathway still
  requires: valid citizenship in the applicant's home country, NOT being a Canadian citizen
  or PR — but critically, entry still requires **prior undergraduate study equivalent to a
  North American bachelor's degree (minimum 3 of 4 years completed) plus MCAT scores**, not a
  post-12th-grade/NEET entry route. This is fundamentally different from the "MBBS" product
  Indian 12th-pass/NEET-qualified students are looking for on this platform.
- Given only 3 seats/year nationally (not India-specific), a years-long prior undergraduate
  degree prerequisite, MCAT requirement, and no NEET/12th-pass entry option, this cannot be
  honestly presented as an "MBBS" program for our audience.
- **Decision: omit.** No `mbbs` program listed.

Sources:
- [Schulich Medicine & Dentistry Admissions — application pathways / international pathway](https://www.schulich.uwo.ca/med-dent-admissions/medicine/application-pathways.html)
- [Frequently Asked Questions — Medicine Admissions](https://www.schulich.uwo.ca/med_dent_admissions/medicine/frequently_asked_questions.html)
- Secondary corroboration: [BeMo Academic Consulting — Schulich School of Medicine: How to Get In](https://bemoacademicconsulting.com/blog/schulich-school-of-medicine)

### DDS / Dentistry (maps to `bds` course slug in our catalog)

- Up to **four (4) seats/year are available for international applicants**, but they must meet
  the **same admission requirements as domestic applicants**: minimum cGPA 3.0 across a
  completed (or nearly completed) North American-style undergraduate degree (≥90 credit
  hours/3 years), DAT score, Casper score, prerequisite coursework, autobiographical sketch.
- Explicitly, **"internationally trained dentists are NOT eligible to apply or enter the DDS
  Program."** Graduates of non-CDA-recognized dental programs may instead apply to the
  **Advanced Standing Program for International Dental Graduates (ASPIDG)** — a different,
  separate credentialing/upgrade pathway, not a fresh-entry BDS-equivalent degree.
- This means: (a) it is not a 12th-pass entry program, (b) requires DAT/Casper plus a
  completed undergrad degree, and (c) is only 4 seats/year globally, not specifically
  accessible to Indian NEET-stream applicants as an "BDS" pathway.
- **Decision: omit.** No `bds` program listed.

Source: [Dentistry Admissions — Frequently Asked Questions (International applicants section)](https://www.schulich.uwo.ca/med-dent-admissions/dentistry/frequently-asked-questions.html)

### Medical PG / residency (maps to `medical-pg` course slug in our catalog)

- Postgraduate residency training (CaRMS match) for International Medical Graduates (IMGs) at
  Schulich is explicitly defined for candidates who are **already Canadian citizens or
  permanent residents** who obtained their MD outside Canada.
- A separate "Internationally Sponsored Residents and Fellows" pathway exists, but this is a
  government/institution-to-government-sponsorship arrangement (e.g., a sponsoring country or
  agency funds a trainee's placement) — not a general open-application pathway for individual
  Indian doctors.
- **Decision: omit.** No `medical-pg` program listed.

Sources:
- [International Medical Graduate (IMG) — Postgraduate Medical Education](https://www.schulich.uwo.ca/medicine/postgraduate/future_learners/international_medical_graduates.html)
- [Internationally Sponsored Residents and Fellows](https://www.schulich.uwo.ca/medicine/postgraduate/future_learners/internationally_sponsored_residents_and_fellows/index.html)

### Nursing / Pharmacy (`bsc-nursing`, `pharmacy` course slugs)

- No standalone BSc Nursing or Pharmacy degree program was found under the Schulich School of
  Medicine & Dentistry umbrella. (Western University as a whole has a separate Faculty of
  Health Sciences with a nursing school, but that is a distinct faculty from Schulich Medicine
  & Dentistry — the specific WDOMS queue entry here is scoped to "Schulich School of Medicine &
  Dentistry," so this was not researched as a Schulich-affiliated program.)
- **Decision: omit** — out of scope for this queue entry / not corroborated as a Schulich
  program.

## Overall conclusion

Western University's Schulich School of Medicine & Dentistry does not have a program that can
be honestly marketed to Indian NEET/12th-pass students under any of this platform's course
categories. Publishing a university page here would require either fabricating a pathway or
badly mischaracterizing a highly restrictive, citizenship-gated Canadian professional program
as an accessible "study abroad" option — both against the sourcing/no-fabrication rules for
this project.

**Recommendation**: Do not publish. Mark the WDOMS/research-queue entry as held/rejected with
this record as the justification. If Western University's admissions policies change in the
future (e.g., an expanded international pathway with NEET/12th-pass entry), this file can be
revisited.
