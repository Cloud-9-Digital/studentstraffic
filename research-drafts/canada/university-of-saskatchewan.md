## Programs included (publish-ready)

### 1. Bachelor of Science in Nursing (B.S.N.) — courseSlug: `bsc-nursing`
- 4-year program (Year 1 = pre-professional prerequisite year; Years 2-4 = College of Nursing, admission is competitive)
- CASN-accredited; CRNS-approved (Sept 2023, 7-year approval)
- **Confirmed international-friendly:** College of Nursing's admissions page explicitly states "Canadian or international residency status is not considered in the application process" for standard applicants, and a separate International Students page states international students are "welcomed."
- Leads to NCLEX-RN eligibility + CRNS registration.
- Tuition (USask official Year-2 estimate, 2026-2027 CAD rates): International ~CAD 48,305 tuition + ~CAD 1,484 fees + ~CAD 1,500 books = ~CAD 51,289 total. Canadian-resident estimate for comparison: ~CAD 12,964 total.
- English proficiency: TOEFL iBT (min sub-scores listed), IELTS 7 overall (6/6/7/6 sub-scores), CAEL 70, OR proof of 3+ years English-medium secondary study (India's CBSE and IGCSE explicitly listed as accepted curricula) plus 24 CU of prerequisite coursework at an English-medium post-secondary institution.
- Sources: https://admissions.usask.ca/nursing.php, https://nursing.usask.ca/globalinitiatives/international-students.php

### 2. Doctor of Pharmacy (Pharm.D.) — courseSlug: `pharmacy`
- Requires 2 years (60 credit units) of prerequisite coursework before the 4-year professional program — effectively a 6-year pathway.
- 90 first-year seats total. **Only up to 9 of the 90 seats** are reserved for "non-Saskatchewan residents," a pool covering BOTH out-of-province Canadians AND all international applicants combined. This makes it an extremely seat-scarce route for Indian applicants (included honestly, with a strong caveat, rather than omitted, since it is a real and legitimate — if narrow — pathway).
- Tuition (USask official estimate, 2026-2027 CAD): Years 1-2 international ~CAD 73,618 tuition (vs ~CAD 24,539 Canadian-resident); Years 3-4 ~CAD 21,679 for all students regardless of residency. Plus ~CAD 1,332 fees + ~CAD 2,000 books/year.
- Admission ranking: Academic record 75%, Test of Critical Skills (Casper), Personal Video Interview 25% combined weighting with Casper.
- License path: PEBC qualifying exam + provincial registration.
- Source: https://admissions.usask.ca/pharmacy.php

## City / context facts (corroborated, multi-source)

- **Population:** Saskatoon urban population ~257,000 (searched web aggregate; consistent across sources).
- **International student body:** USask hosts 26,000+ students from 130+ countries, including ~3,000 international students (aggregated search result, consistent with USask's own international-student messaging).
- **Crime / safety (important honest caveat, NOT omitted):**
  - CBC News: "Saskatoon improves ranking on crime severity index but still sits in top 10" — Saskatoon ranked 8th of 41 Canadian metro areas on the Statistics Canada Crime Severity Index in the most recent reporting cycle (improved from 6th previously). https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/saskatoon/saskatoon-police-stats-can-crime-severity-index-1.7276528
  - CBC News: "Saskatchewan tops StatsCan crime severity list for 25th year in a row" — Saskatchewan has ranked #1 among Canadian provinces on the same index every year since 1998. https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/saskatchewan/saskatchewan-tops-statscan-crime-severity-list-for-25th-year-in-a-row-1.7275650
  - This was included as an honest "things to consider" caveat rather than suppressed, per the sourcing rules (no cherry-picking only flattering facts).
- **Indian/Hindu/Sikh community:** Lakshmi Narayan Temple, run by the Hindu Society of Saskatchewan (est. 1985), per The Encyclopedia of Saskatchewan (https://esask.uregina.ca/entry/hindu_communities.html) — offers aarti, satsang, Hindi classes. Active Sikh community holds an annual Vaisakhi Sikh Day Parade with langar. Multiple Indian restaurants and grocery options confirmed via general web search aggregation (DesiYXE / Wanderlog / SkipTheDishes listings) — used only to support "food/community exists," not for precise counts.
- **Housing:** Voyageur Place is the residence recommended for first-year/international/exchange students (tunnel access to campus, meal plan at Marquis Culinary Centre); first-year guarantee applies for direct-from-high-school applicants aged ≤25 without dependents who apply and pay by Jan 31. Source: https://livewithus.usask.ca/incomingresidents/whocanliveinresidence.php

## Regulator framing used

- Nursing: NCLEX-RN (Canada) + CRNS (Saskatchewan provincial regulator) — correctly Canadian, NOT the Indian Nursing Council. FAQ explicitly tells students that returning to practise in India would require separately verifying INC recognition of a Canadian degree — no false regulator claim made.
- Pharmacy: PEBC (Pharmacy Examining Board of Canada) qualifying exam + provincial registration — correct Canadian regulator, not PCI (India's regulator), since this is a Canadian-practice pathway.
- Medicine: no program included, so no regulator claim needed/made.
