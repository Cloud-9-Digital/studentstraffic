# Research record: Universite Laval — Faculte de medecine (Quebec City, Canada)

Status: **HELD — not published, gate not met (ok=false)**

Researched: 2026-07-07

## Why this draft is held

This platform's university pages are structured around a fixed set of India/NEET-audience
course slugs: `mbbs`, `medical-pg`, `bsc-nursing`, `pharmacy`, `bds`. Each published program
must have an honest, currently-open pathway that an Indian student could realistically apply to
under one of those slugs, with an explicit official program URL.

After multi-source research (official Faculte de medecine pages, the official Universite Laval
program catalog entry for the M.D., and an independent secondary source), **no such pathway
exists at Universite Laval's Faculte de medecine** for any of the platform's course categories.
Details below. Per the hard "exhaustive-then-omit, never fabricate" rule, this draft is held
rather than published with an invented or misleading program.

## Key facts (corroborated)

- **Institution**: Universite Laval, Faculte de medecine (Faculty of Medicine).
- **Founded**: The Faculte de medecine was founded in 1854 — the first francophone medical
  faculty in Canada / North America (first dean Dr. Jean Blanchet inaugurated courses on
  September 25, 1854). Universite Laval itself traces its roots to the Seminaire de Quebec
  (1663) and became a university in 1852.
- **Type**: Public research university (Quebec, Canada).
- **City**: Quebec City (Quebec provincial capital), main campus in the Sainte-Foy–Sillery–
  Cap-Rouge borough.
- **Scale**: Universite Laval enrolls roughly 42,500+ students overall (57,000+ in 2021-22
  counting all registration types), with international students/permanent residents making up
  close to 15% of the student community — but this is a university-wide figure, not specific to
  the M.D. program (see below for why the M.D. is effectively closed to Indian applicants).
- **Official website**: https://www.fmed.ulaval.ca/accueil

## Program-by-program findings

### M.D. — Doctorat de 1er cycle en medecine (maps to `mbbs` course slug in our catalog)

- The official program page (`ulaval.ca/etudes/programmes/doctorat-en-medecine`) states the
  program is **200 credits, 4 to 5 years**, taught across Quebec City, Levis, and Rimouski
  teaching sites, with **301 places available for Quebecoises et Quebecois, a number fixed by
  government decree ("nombre fixe par decret gouvernemental")**, and a minimum admission
  "Cote R" of 33 for regular CEGEP applicants.
- **Language of instruction: French only.** Universite Laval's Faculte de medecine is
  explicitly one of the French-language-only medical schools in Quebec — all coursework,
  clinical rotations, and patient interactions are conducted in French. No English-medium M.D.
  pathway exists.
- **Admission prerequisite: Quebec's DEC (Diplome d'etudes collegiales)** in Sciences de la
  nature (or a small set of recognized equivalents: DEC in Sciences, Lettres et Arts; IB
  Diploma obtained in Quebec with satisfactory science coursework; New Brunswick DSS under a
  specific intergovernmental agreement). This is a Quebec/Canada-specific CEGEP-system
  qualification — an Indian Class 12 / NEET-qualifying certificate is not a listed equivalent
  route on the official admissibility page.
- **International-applicant quota is effectively a single seat.** Secondary corroboration
  (BeMo Academic Consulting's roundup of Quebec medical schools) confirms Laval "typically
  admits only 1 candidate from other Canadian provinces and territories and 1 candidate from
  the international applicant pool" per year — i.e., a nationally-scoped quota of one
  international seat across the entire world's applicant pool, layered on top of the French-
  language and DEC-equivalency requirements above.
- Combined effect: even the tiny international quota requires (a) fluent academic French, (b)
  a DEC-equivalent science qualification recognized by Laval (not a NEET/12th-pass Indian
  qualification per se), and (c) competing for effectively one seat/year worldwide. This cannot
  be honestly marketed as an accessible "MBBS abroad" pathway for Indian Class 12/NEET-qualified
  students.
- **Decision: omit.** No `mbbs` program listed.

### Medical PG / residency (maps to `medical-pg` course slug in our catalog)

- Postgraduate medical training (residency/fellowship) at Laval's Faculte de medecine is
  governed by the same French-language requirement, and licensure to practise in Quebec is
  administered by the **Collège des médecins du Québec (CMQ)**, which first requires either a
  Quebec M.D. degree or formal equivalency recognition of a foreign M.D. before a candidate can
  even apply to a residency program (CaRMS-style match). This is a credentialing/equivalency
  pathway for **already-qualified physicians**, not a postgraduate-degree study option that an
  Indian medical graduate could enroll in directly — and no explicit open international
  residency-application route was found.
- **Decision: omit.** No `medical-pg` program listed.

Source: [Faculte de medecine — Candidates et candidats de l'international ("Exercer au Quebec" / CMQ licensure section)](https://www.fmed.ulaval.ca/candidates-et-candidats-de-linternational)

### Nursing / Pharmacy / Dentistry (`bsc-nursing`, `pharmacy`, `bds` course slugs)

- The Faculte de medecine's official international-candidates and program pages reference
  research stays, student exchanges, and full health-sciences programs of study broadly, but
  no standalone BSc Nursing, Pharmacy, or Dentistry (BDS-equivalent) degree program was found
  administered by, or clearly badged under, the Faculte de medecine specifically. (Universite
  Laval as a whole has separate faculties for some health disciplines; this queue entry is
  scoped to the Faculte de medecine, so those were not researched as part of this entry.)
- **Decision: omit** — not corroborated as a Faculte-de-medecine program within scope.

## Regulator framing (not asserted)

No NMC/NEET/FMGE, PCI, or INC framing is asserted anywhere in this record, since no admissible
program exists to attach such framing to. Practice licensure in Quebec runs through the Collège
des médecins du Québec (CMQ) — noted above only as a corroborated fact about the postgraduate/
licensure pathway, not as a claim relevant to an Indian-student enrolment product.

## Overall conclusion

Universite Laval's Faculte de medecine does not have a program that can be honestly marketed to
Indian NEET/12th-pass students under any of this platform's course categories. The M.D. program
is French-language-only, gated behind Quebec's CEGEP/DEC academic system, subject to a
government-decreed quota overwhelmingly reserved for Quebec residents, and open to only about
one international seat per year worldwide. Publishing a page here would require either
fabricating a pathway or badly mischaracterizing a highly restrictive, language- and
credential-gated Canadian professional program as an accessible "study abroad" option — both
against the sourcing/no-fabrication rules for this project.

**Recommendation**: Do not publish. Mark the research-queue entry as held/rejected with this
record as the justification. If Laval's admissions policies materially change in the future
(e.g., an English-medium track or an expanded international pathway with NEET/12th-pass entry),
this file can be revisited.
