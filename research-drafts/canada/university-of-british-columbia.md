## Key facts (corroborated, multi-source)

- Public research university, founded 1908 by an Act of the British Columbia provincial legislature; Vancouver (Point Grey) campus officially opened in 1925. Second campus in the Okanagan (Kelowna). Confirmed on ubc.ca, cross-checked against Wikipedia and The Canadian Encyclopedia.
- UBC is a large, globally ranked multi-faculty university -- not a niche medical/dental school.

## Program-by-program access findings for Indian/international students (this is the core research finding)

This university does **not** work like a typical India-outbound MBBS/nursing/pharmacy destination. Each health-professional program was checked individually:

| Program | International-applicant access | Source |
|---|---|---|
| MD (Medicine) | **Closed.** Only Canadian citizens, permanent residents, or persons with Canadian refugee status may apply. | mdprogram.med.ubc.ca admission-requirements page |
| BSN Nursing (Vancouver) | **Closed.** Same citizenship/PR/refugee-status restriction. | UBC Academic Calendar / nursing.ubc.ca |
| PharmD (Entry-to-Practice) | **Unconfirmed for direct international entry.** Admissions pages describe eligibility for Canadian citizens/PRs with BC/territory-resident preference; no direct international pathway is described, so it is *omitted* rather than asserted open or closed. | pharmsci.ubc.ca admissions-information page |
| DMD (Doctor of Dental Medicine) | **Open.** Faculty of Dentistry states the international applicant pool is assessed using the same criteria as the domestic pool. | dentistry.ubc.ca admission-procedures + FAQ pages |

Because of this, **DMD is the only program included in the publish-ready draft**, mapped to the existing course taxonomy's `bds` slug as the closest match (an editorial mapping choice, since UBC has no program literally named "BDS" -- the draft content says this explicitly).

## DMD (Doctor of Dental Medicine) program detail

- **Duration:** 4 years
- **Medium:** English (TOEFL/IELTS required if prior education wasn't in a primarily English-medium system)
- **Entry route:** NOT direct-from-high-school. Requires ~90 transferable university credits (~3 years of prior post-secondary study), minimum 70% average / 2.8 GPA, specific prerequisite courses.
- **Entrance test:** Canadian/American DAT (Dental Aptitude Test) -- **not NEET**. Recent cycle: DAT writing deadline 31 Aug 2026.
- **Application system:** ADEA AADSAS (centralized North American dental-school application service). DMD Class of 2031 cycle: applications open 12 May 2026, submissions open 3 June 2026, application deadline 10 Oct 2026, documents deadline 23 Oct 2026, AADSAS verification deadline 31 Oct 2026.
- **Seats:** Roughly 60-70 total DMD seats per class; a limited, unspecified number go to international applicants (exact international quota not publicly stated).
- **Post-graduation licensure:** NDEB (National Dental Examining Board of Canada) certification exams -- a Canadian licensing pathway, not an Indian one.
- **Tuition:** NOT confirmed as a single headline figure. UBC's own "DMD International Student Fees" page defers to the UBC Academic Calendar for the current year's tuition rate rather than stating it directly on that page. Confirmed *other* estimated costs from that page: textbooks ~CAD 1,000-2,100/year, surgical loupes/lights ~CAD 3,000 (year 1 only), NDEB certification fee ~CAD 2,500 (year 4 only), accident insurance ~CAD 7/year.
  - **This is why tuition was OMITTED from the draft** rather than estimated or fabricated -- the source didn't give a clean confirmed number, and the page itself points elsewhere (Academic Calendar) for the authoritative figure.
- **Financial aid:** Faculty of Dentistry states scholarships/bursaries for international students are limited -- self-financing is expected.
- **Separate closed pathway (caveat, not offered):** The "Internationally-Trained Dentists" IDDCP (International Dental Degree Completion Program) is explicitly **not accepting applications until further notice** per the Faculty's own page. This is mentioned as a caveat only, not offered as a route.

## City / context grounding (Vancouver)

- Ranked among Canada's safest and most livable cities; QS Best Student Cities has ranked Vancouver as Canada's top student city.
- Large, well-established South Asian community. Historic hub: the **Punjabi Market** district on Main Street, Vancouver (Sunset neighbourhood), serving the community with Indian restaurants, sweet shops and grocers since the 1970s (Destination Vancouver, Wikipedia). Since the 1980s-2000s the community has increasingly suburbanised toward **Surrey**, which now also has extensive Indian grocery/restaurant options.
- UBC Vancouver houses ~14,000 students across 15 residences (first-year halls like Totem Park/Place Vanier; apartment-style like Marine Drive/Walter Gage for upper-year/grad students). On-campus housing is **not guaranteed** beyond the first-year guarantee cohort -- relevant caveat for a 4-year DMD student.
- UBC has a dedicated International Student Advising office (RCIC/RISIA-staffed) in the UBC Life Building for visa, health insurance, and settling-in support.

## What was OMITTED and why

1. **MBBS / medical-pg / bsc-nursing / pharmacy courseSlugs** -- omitted entirely. UBC's MD and BSN-Vancouver programs are confirmed closed to international applicants; PharmD's international-applicant status could not be confirmed either way (not asserted).
2. **Exact DMD international tuition figure** -- omitted. UBC's own international-fees page does not state it directly; it refers to the Academic Calendar. No number was fabricated; feeNotes explains this and directs students to confirm with UBC Enrolment Services.
3. **Exact number of international seats in the DMD program** -- omitted (search results indicated seats vary year to year and are not broken out publicly by domestic/international split beyond noting a "limited number" of international admits).
4. **Any Indian regulator claim (NMC/PCI/INC/DCI)** -- deliberately not asserted for this Canadian degree. Draft explicitly tells students this is a Canadian licensure pathway (NDEB) and to verify Indian-recognition needs themselves if planning to return and practise in India.
5. **IDDCP internationally-trained-dentist pathway** -- not offered as a route since it is confirmed closed to new applications; mentioned only as an honest caveat.
