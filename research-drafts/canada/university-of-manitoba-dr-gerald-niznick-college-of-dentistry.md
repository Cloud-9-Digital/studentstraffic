# Research record: University of Manitoba (Dr. Gerald Niznick College of Dentistry)

**Status: HELD - not gate-passing.** This draft is NOT publish-ready. Saved as a local research
record so the overnight run does not re-research this school, and so a human can review the
reasoning below.

## Key facts (corroborated, multi-source)

- **Institution**: University of Manitoba, Dr. Gerald Niznick College of Dentistry
- **City**: Winnipeg, Manitoba, Canada
- **Type**: Public university
- **Established**: The College of Dentistry traces to 1958 (per college history references cited
  across UM materials); the University of Manitoba itself was founded in 1877 as Canada's first
  university.
- **Official site**: https://umanitoba.ca/dentistry/
- **Faculty structure**: Part of the Rady Faculty of Health Sciences.
- **Flagship program**: Doctor of Dental Medicine (DMD) — 4-year entry-level program.
- **International Dentist Degree Program (IDDP)**: A separate 2 to 2.5-year program for
  internationally trained dentists.

## Why this could not be published on this platform (India/NEET-audience gate)

This platform's course catalog for dentistry is `bds` — an **entry-level** Bachelor of Dental
Surgery-equivalent track for students who have not yet earned a dental degree (the NEET-UG
audience). The gate requires an honest, explicit `courseSlug` mapping for each program.

Research into University of Manitoba's actual admission pathways found:

1. **Entry-level DMD (4-year program)**: Admission is restricted to **Canadian Citizens and
   Permanent Residents**. Applicants born outside Canada must submit proof of Canadian
   citizenship, Permanent Residence, or Refugee status as a mandatory application document.
   There is no route for a general international applicant (e.g., an Indian student straight out
   of high school/pre-dental studies) to enter this 4-year DMD as a fresh student.
   Source: https://umanitoba.ca/explore/undergraduate-admissions/requirements/dentistry

2. **International Dentist Degree Program (IDDP)**: This is the only pathway explicitly open to
   "International applicants" (it has a distinct, higher application fee for international
   applicants vs. Canadian citizens/PRs, confirming it does admit non-PR/citizens). However,
   eligibility requires the applicant to:
   - Already be a graduate of a **minimum four-year university dental degree program** not
     accredited by the Commission on Dental Accreditation of Canada (i.e., already hold a
     BDS/DDS-equivalent qualification from another country — this is a program for practicing or
     newly-graduated dentists, not first-time dental students).
   - Have completed the ADA's Advanced Dental Admissions Test (ADAT).
   - Have taken/passed NDEB's Assessment of Clinical Judgment (ACJ) and National Dental
     Examination of Clinical Competence (NDECC) examinations.
   - Successful applicants enter directly into **Year 3** of the DMD program (a 2 to 2.5-year
     "degree completion/upgrading" track), not year 1.
   Source: https://umanitoba.ca/explore/undergraduate-admissions/requirements/iddp
   Source (program structure/catalog entry): https://catalog.umanitoba.ca/undergraduate-studies/health-sciences/dentistry/dental-medicine-dmd-international-dentists-degree-program/

Because IDDP is a **degree-completion program for already-qualified dentists** (not an entry-level
BDS-equivalent program for NEET-stage students), mapping it to the `bds` course slug would
misrepresent the offering to Indian students who are looking for a first dental degree. This is
the same structural situation found and HELD for Western University's Schulich School of Medicine
& Dentistry (see `western-university-schulich-school-of-medicine-dentistry.md` in this same
folder) — Canadian dental schools generally reserve entry-level DMD/DDS seats for
citizens/permanent residents, and only offer upgrading pathways (IDDP-style) to internationally
trained dentists.

No other entry-level program (MBBS-equivalent medicine, BSc Nursing, or Pharmacy) exists at the
Dr. Gerald Niznick College of Dentistry — it is a dentistry-only college — so there was no
alternative course-slug mapping to explore at this institution.

## Recommendation

Hold this school. Do not publish a page for it under the `bds` course slug. If the platform later
adds a course slug/category for "foreign-trained dentist upgrading/licensure programs," IDDP could
be revisited as a legitimate (but niche, post-qualification) offering — but it would need very
different framing (aimed at Indian dentists who already hold a BDS and want Canadian licensure,
not at NEET-stage students).
