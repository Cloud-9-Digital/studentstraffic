## Why this draft is held

This platform's university pages are structured around a fixed set of India/NEET-audience
course slugs: `mbbs`, `medical-pg`, `bsc-nursing`, `pharmacy`, `bds`. Each published program
must have an honest, currently-open pathway that an Indian student could realistically apply to
under one of those slugs, with an explicit official program URL.

After multi-source research (official Faculte de medecine et des sciences de la sante pages,
the official Universite de Sherbrooke admission/program catalog entry for the Doctorat en
medecine, and independent secondary sources), **no such pathway exists at Universite de
Sherbrooke's Faculte de medecine et des sciences de la sante** for any of the platform's course
categories. Details below. Per the hard "exhaustive-then-omit, never fabricate" rule, this
draft is held rather than published with an invented or misleading program.

## Key facts (corroborated)

- **Institution**: Universite de Sherbrooke, Faculte de medecine et des sciences de la sante
  (Faculty of Medicine and Health Sciences).
- **University founded**: 1954 (Universite de Sherbrooke). The Faculty of Medicine itself was
  announced in February 1961 by Mgr Irenee Pinard, rector of the university, with the first
  medical students beginning studies in September 1966.
- **Type**: Public French-language research university (Quebec, Canada).
- **City**: Sherbrooke, Quebec — a major university city roughly 150 km from Montreal and
  220 km from Quebec City, about 40 km from the US border; roughly 20% of the city's population
  is in post-secondary education. The Faculty's Health Campus in Sherbrooke hosts medicine,
  health sciences, and biomedical research; the Faculty also has a presence in Longueuil,
  Saguenay–Lac-Saint-Jean, and Moncton (New Brunswick).
- **Scale**: Universite de Sherbrooke enrolls roughly 31,000+ students overall, including more
  than 2,700–3,500 international students from 97–98 countries and territories across its three
  campuses — university-wide figures, not specific to the M.D. program (see below for why the
  M.D. is effectively closed to Indian applicants despite the university's broader international
  presence).
- **Recognition**: The Faculty of Medicine and Health Sciences was designated a WHO
  collaborating centre in 2001, reportedly the first Canadian and francophone medical school so
  recognized for health-professions training; it is one of four medical schools in Quebec.
- **Official website**: https://www.usherbrooke.ca/medecine/

## Program-by-program findings

### M.D. — Doctorat en medecine (maps to `mbbs` course slug in our catalog)

- The official admission page (`usherbrooke.ca/admission/programme/238/doctorat-en-medecine/`)
  and the official international-quota page
  (`usherbrooke.ca/etudes-medecine/programmes-detudes/doctorat-en-medecine/admission/contingent-international`)
  describe a **"contingent international"** — a small, separately-managed international quota
  within the overall M.D. cohort — rather than open, unlimited international admission.
- **Language of instruction: French only.** Independent secondary sources (BeMo Academic
  Consulting's dedicated "Sherbrooke Medical School" guide and its Quebec-wide medical-schools
  roundup) confirm Sherbrooke teaches exclusively in French, and that **the CASPer situational-
  judgment admissions test must itself be taken in French** (Health Sciences version) — an
  additional, non-negotiable French-fluency gate on top of coursework and clinical French.
  Candidates must also demonstrate C1-level French proficiency (average across four
  competencies) by August 15 of the admission year per official program information surfaced in
  search results.
- **Admission prerequisite: Quebec's DEC (Diplome d'etudes collegiales) or DSS**, or
  alternatively at least 90 university credits in a single first-cycle program (or being on
  track to complete a bachelor's degree before the academic year begins). This is a
  Quebec/Canada-specific CEGEP-system qualification pathway; an Indian Class 12 / NEET-qualifying
  certificate is not a listed equivalent route in the official admissibility criteria surfaced.
- **Government-set quota overwhelmingly reserved for Quebec/Maritime residents.** Secondary
  corroboration (BeMo) states roughly 80% of Sherbrooke's M.D. seats are reserved for Quebec
  residents and about 12% for Maritime-province residents (New Brunswick, Nova Scotia, Prince
  Edward Island), leaving only a handful of seats for all other Canadian, US, and international
  French-speaking applicants combined — typically **around 1 seat for Western Canada and 1 seat
  for international (visa-holding) applicants per year**.
- **International-applicant acceptance is extremely narrow.** Secondary sources report that in
  a recent cycle only 1 international student enrolled out of 15 international applicants in one
  reporting, and a wider figure elsewhere cites roughly 5 admitted out of 91 international
  applicants (~5.5% acceptance) — consistently describing an effectively single-digit,
  often single-seat, international intake per year worldwide.
- **International applicants are not required to travel to Sherbrooke for interviews** (video
  interviews are used instead) — noted as a minor accessibility point, but it does not offset
  the French-fluency and quota constraints above.
- Combined effect: even the tiny international quota requires (a) fluent academic/clinical
  French verified via a French-language CASPer test and C1 proficiency, (b) a DEC-equivalent or
  90-credit qualification pathway not aligned to Indian Class 12/NEET qualifications, and (c)
  competing for approximately one seat/year worldwide at roughly 5–7% acceptance among that
  narrow pool. This cannot be honestly marketed as an accessible "MBBS abroad" pathway for
  Indian Class 12/NEET-qualified students.
- **Decision: omit.** No `mbbs` program listed.

### Medical PG / residency (maps to `medical-pg` course slug in our catalog)

- Postgraduate medical training (residency/fellowship) at Sherbrooke's Faculte de medecine is
  governed by the same French-language requirement, and licensure to practise in Quebec is
  administered by the **Collège des médecins du Québec (CMQ)**, which requires either a Quebec
  M.D. degree or formal equivalency recognition of a foreign M.D. before a candidate can apply
  to a residency program (CaRMS-style match). This is a credentialing/equivalency pathway for
  **already-qualified physicians**, not a postgraduate-degree study option an Indian medical
  graduate could enroll in directly — and no explicit open international residency-application
  route was found in the official pages surfaced.
- **Decision: omit.** No `medical-pg` program listed.

Source: [Etudiants internationaux — Faculte de medecine et des sciences de la sante (official)](https://www.usherbrooke.ca/medecine/international/etudiants-internationaux)

### Nursing / Pharmacy / Dentistry (`bsc-nursing`, `pharmacy`, `bds` course slugs)

- Search results confirm the Faculty offers Nursing, Rehabilitation Therapy, and other
  health-profession programs alongside medicine, but **all instruction across the Faculty is in
  French** (per the official international-students page and secondary sources), and no
  standalone English-medium or internationally-accessible BSc Nursing, Pharmacy, or Dentistry
  (BDS-equivalent) admission pathway comparable to the platform's course catalog was found. No
  official page surfaced described an open, India/NEET-relevant international-student track for
  these programs.
- **Decision: omit** — not corroborated as an accessible pathway within scope.

## Overall conclusion

Universite de Sherbrooke's Faculte de medecine et des sciences de la sante does not have a
program that can be honestly marketed to Indian NEET/12th-pass students under any of this
platform's course categories. The M.D. program is French-language-only (with a French-language
CASPer requirement), gated behind Quebec's CEGEP/DEC academic system or a 90-credit equivalency,
subject to a quota overwhelmingly reserved for Quebec and Maritime residents, and open to only
roughly one international seat per year worldwide at a single-digit acceptance rate among that
narrow applicant pool. Publishing a page here would require either fabricating a pathway or
badly mischaracterizing a highly restrictive, language- and credential-gated Canadian
professional program as an accessible "study abroad" option — both against the
sourcing/no-fabrication rules for this project. This mirrors the same structural finding already
documented for Universite Laval's Faculte de medecine (also held) — Quebec's provincially-funded
medical schools are not, at present, a fit for this platform's India-audience MBBS/PG/nursing/
pharmacy/dentistry catalog.
