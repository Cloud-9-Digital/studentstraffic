## Key facts (corroborated, multi-source)

- **Institution**: University of Vlora "Ismail Qemali" (Universiteti "Ismail Qemali" i Vlorës) — public Albanian university.
- **Founded**: 28 February 1994 (Wikipedia; also referenced as the founding date/legal basis — VKM/Council of Ministers Decision No.105, dt. 28-02-1994 — in the official Nursing Department curriculum regulation and confirmed independently on the ASCAL licensing record).
- **Structure**: Originally opened with three faculties, including a dedicated Faculty of Nursing. Today organized into four faculties: Faculty of Technical Sciences (FSHTN), Faculty of Health (FSH), Faculty of Humanities (FSHH), Faculty of Economy (FE).
- **Faculty of Health (FSH)** has two departments relevant to nursing/health:
  - **Nursing Department (Departamenti i Infermierisë)** — offers: PD Assistance in Geriatrics (professional diploma), **BSc in General Nursing**, MP in Surgical Nursing, MP in Pathological Nursing, MSc in Nursing Sciences.
  - **Healthcare Department (Departamenti i Kujdesit Shëndetësor)** — offers a separate Bachelor in Midwifery Nursing (Infermieri-Mami) and Master Professional in Pediatric Nursing.
- **Program covered in this draft**: Bachelor in General Nursing (Bcs në Infermieri të Përgjithshme), Nursing Department, Faculty of Health.
  - 3 academic years / 6 semesters, minimum 180 ECTS, diploma "Bcs në Infermieri të Përgjithshme".
  - Approved by the Ministry of Education and Science in 1994 (VKM Nr.105, dt. 28-02-1994), reformatted by MAS Order No.398 (10-08-2011), and most recently reorganized under a 2025-2028 curriculum approved by the UV Academic Senate (Decision No.26, dated 19.03.2025).
  - **Medium of instruction**: Officially listed by the university itself as "Infermieri e përgjithshme (në gjuhën shqipe dhe angleze)" — i.e. Albanian AND English — in the official Matura-admissions ranking PDF for this exact program. The curriculum also includes dedicated professional-English nursing courses (ENGI 130 "Anglisht" / ENGI 131 "Anglisht profesionale"), alongside optional German (GERI 130/131) and Italian (ITAI 130/131) tracks as electives in the "supplementary subjects" block. This is a genuine differentiator from several other Albanian nursing programs that are Italian-medium only (e.g., UNIZKM in Tirana/Elbasan).
  - Facilities: 5 laboratories + 1 Health Training Simulation Centre (with a birth/delivery simulator), per the Healthcare Department page (shared FSH facility described there; the Nursing Department page does not re-describe facilities separately but is part of the same Faculty of Health).
  - Clinical placements: mandatory teaching + professional practice at primary/secondary healthcare institutions; a 10-ECTS "Professional Practice" (INF 300) module plus practicum embedded across clinical modules. FSH materials (via the Healthcare Department page) reference cooperation agreements with a regional hospital network including Vlorë Regional Hospital and Fier Memorial for clinical practice — this reference appears on the Healthcare Department page rather than being repeated verbatim on the Nursing Department page, so it is presented in the draft as a Faculty-of-Health-level arrangement, not confirmed exhaustively for every course.
  - Attendance rules: lectures 70%, seminars 80%, hospital practice 100%, laboratories 100%.
  - Full curriculum (2025-2028 reorganization) totals 180 ECTS across five blocks: Basic (A, 29 ECTS), Characteristic (B, 99 ECTS), Interdisciplinary/integrative (C, 27 ECTS), Supplementary (D, 19 ECTS, includes the language electives + professional practice), Closing obligations (E, 6 ECTS, final exam/diploma).
- **Accreditation**: ASCAL (Albania's Quality Assurance Agency in Higher Education) officially lists the University of Vlora "Ismail Qemali" as an active, licensed, public "University" category HEI (Licensing Document VKM Nr.105, dt. 28.02.1994) and separately lists "Bachelor Infermieri e Përgjithshme" (General Nursing) under "Department of Nursing" and "Bachelor Infermieri-Mami" under "Department of Health Care" among its accredited/registered Bachelor programs.
- **City**: Vlorë — Albania's third-largest city, southern coast where the Adriatic and Ionian Seas meet; northern gateway to the "Albanian Riviera"; relatively low cost of living versus Tirana/Western Europe; tourism/fishing/light-industry economy.

## What was OMITTED and why

- **On-campus hostel/dormitory details**: No dedicated, itemised international-student housing program could be confirmed for the Nursing Department or Faculty of Health. Draft states private rental is the default expectation and recommends contacting the university directly.
- **INC (Indian Nursing Council) / WDOMS-equivalent recognition status**: Could not be confirmed from any source reviewed (official site, ASCAL, Wikipedia, THE profile). No regulatory claim is made anywhere in the draft; students are explicitly told to verify this themselves with INC before applying. This is the single most important caveat for the target Indian-student audience.
- **Indian food / Indian-student community infrastructure in Vlorë**: No evidence found of Indian restaurants, grocers, or an existing Indian-student community in Vlorë specifically (as opposed to Tirana, which has more international infrastructure). Draft treats this honestly as an open/thin area requiring self-catering, rather than inventing details.
- **Complete/current list of partner clinical-placement hospitals**: The Healthcare Department page names Vlorë Regional Hospital and Fier Memorial as examples of a "network of regional hospitals" for clinical practice cooperation, but this could not be verified as the current, complete, or Nursing-Department-specific list, so the draft flags this as something to confirm directly with the department rather than asserting a full list.
- **Detailed course-by-course English vs. Albanian delivery split**: The curriculum PDF confirms the program is officially bilingual and lists dedicated English-language coursework, but does not itemize which of the ~39 modules are delivered in English versus Albanian. Draft recommends applicants confirm this directly.

## Regulator framing note

No NMC/PCI/INC/WDOMS claim of any kind is made in the draft. Because this is a **nursing** (not medical/pharmacy) program, the only potentially relevant Indian regulator would be the **Indian Nursing Council (INC)**. No confirmation of INC recognition or WDOMS-equivalent nursing-school listing could be found for this institution, so the draft explicitly instructs prospective Indian students to verify this directly with INC before applying — consistent with the "exhaustive-then-omit, never fabricate" rule.
