## Key facts (corroborated)

- **Type**: Private university.
- **Established**: Legally recognised 20 June 2014 by decree of the Italian Ministry of Education (Gazzetta Ufficiale). Source: Wikipedia infobox citing the official gazette; corroborated by WDOMS listing ("Year Instruction Started: 2014").
- **Location**: Campus in the municipality of Pieve Emanuele, within the Metropolitan City of Milan; built adjacent to / integrated with Humanitas Research Hospital, whose official address is in Rozzano. Both place names appear in official/derivative sources, so both are referenced.
- **Affiliated hospital**: Humanitas Research Hospital — IRCCS-designated (Istituto di Ricovero e Cura a Carattere Scientifico, Italy's top scientific-clinical-research recognition), JCI-accredited, ranked Italy's top hospital in multiple recent national evaluations (source: medicaltourism review article, cross-checked against hospital's own public profile mentions found in search).
- **Official website**: https://www.hunimed.eu/
- **Regulator/registry listing**: Humanitas University Medical School appears in the World Directory of Medical Schools (WDOMS) — Private, Year Instruction Started 2014, Operational Status: Currently operational. WDOMS is WHO/FAIMER-affiliated and used by multiple countries' medical regulators (including bodies that reference it for foreign-degree verification). URL: https://search.wdoms.org/home/SchoolDetail/F0003269

## Program captured

**Medicine and Surgery** (Single-Cycle Master's Degree, English-taught)
- Duration: 6 years
- Medium: English (CEFR B2 recommended for admission)
- Seats: 200/year total — 150 EU, 50 non-EU (official medicine course page)
- Admission test: HUMAT (Hunimed's own test, NOT the national IMAT) — open in two rounds, best score counts
- Curriculum: theory + simulation from year 1 (dedicated Simulation Center); structured clinical clerkships begin year 3, run under the RIME pedagogical framework overseen by Hunimed's Office of Medical Education (OME)
- Tuition (CONFIRMED, official, current): **EUR 23,206/year for non-EU students**, a.y. 2026/2027, per the official Fees & Scholarships page. Does not include accommodation/food/books.
  - EU students are charged on an income-banded ISEP Index scale (EUR 10,206 - 23,206/year depending on family-income category) — not a single figure, so not asserted as one number in the draft; explained honestly instead.
  - Merit scholarships (e.g., Fondazione Rocca) can reduce cost to a flat EUR 5,206/year for top-ranked candidates — limited/competitive, noted as such.
- Intake: September (standard Italian academic year start; HUMAT sessions run earlier in the year)

Course slug mapped: `mbbs` (per the seeding script's course taxonomy — this is the MD/Medicine-and-Surgery program).

## Accommodation

Mario Luzzatto Student House — opened 2018, operated with Camplus, located on the university campus itself. ~240 beds across 62 apartments + 23 rooms (single/double rooms, mini-apartments), private bathrooms, shared kitchens/living rooms, communal bar/restaurant/gym/games room. Fees cover utilities, wifi, AC, weekly linen change, 24h staffed reception. Allocated via annual Camplus application. Students awarded Italy's regional study grant (borsa di diritto allo studio regionale) and not resident in the Milan metro area are guaranteed a shared room as part of that grant.

## City / safety / Indian-community context

- Milan ranks favourably on independent safety indices (Economist safety index, ISTAT/city data) vs other large European cities; main practical risk is petty theft/pickpocketing in crowded transit/tourist areas, not violent crime.
- Campus location (Pieve Emanuele/Rozzano) is a quieter suburban setting outside central Milan.
- Student cost of living in Milan: roughly EUR 1,100-1,800/month excluding tuition (University Living guide).
- Indian community: Italy's Ministry of Labour and Social Policies estimated ~162,000 Indian-origin residents nationally (2023 report), about a third in Lombardy (Milan's region). Indian groceries (e.g., Krishna Indian Bazaar, Porta Venezia) and restaurants (Rangoli since 1997, Saravanaa Bhavan, others) are well established. Student WhatsApp/Facebook groups commonly used for home-cook tiffin services.

## What was OMITTED and why

1. **University-specific NMC/FMGE-NExT recognition claim** — Several third-party admissions-consultancy sites (rmcedu.com, edufever.com, medico-abroad.com, blog.rmgoe.org) assert that Humanitas/Italian MBBS degrees are "recognised by NMC" or list Hunimed as NMC-affiliated. These are commercial student-recruitment sites, not an official Indian regulator source, and no NMC-published, university-specific confirmation was found. Per the sourcing rules (never assert regulator claims unless corroborated from an official/regulator-adjacent source), this was **not asserted as fact**. Instead, the draft states the corroborated fact (WDOMS listing, WHO-affiliated registry) and explicitly tells students to verify NMC/FMGE-NExT treatment themselves. This appears in `thingsToConsider` and the relevant FAQ.
2. **EU-student single tuition figure** — Not stated as one number because the official fee page presents EU fees on an ISEP-Index income band (4 tiers, EUR 10,206–23,206/year), not a flat rate. Full band explained honestly in `feeNotes` instead of collapsing to a fabricated single figure.
3. **WHO / GMC / ECFMG "accredited by" claims from secondary sources** — WebSearch summaries (aggregated, not an official Hunimed page or WHO page directly viewed) claimed Hunimed is "recognised by the WHO" and "accredited by the medical councils of the UK, Ireland, US, Canada, Australia." I could not independently pull an official Hunimed accreditation page or a WHO page confirming this directly (the official "who-we-are" page content fetched did not contain this text in the extracted portion). The one piece I COULD independently verify via direct fetch was the **WDOMS listing** (which is the operative WHO-affiliated registry mechanism), so the draft leans on that verified fact rather than repeating the broader multi-country accreditation claim, which was left out as insufficiently corroborated from a primary source in this session.
4. **Specific HUMAT test dates/format for a given intake year** — Left as "check the official Apply page" rather than citing a specific date that will go stale.
5. **Exact scholarship seat counts for non-EU students in the current cycle** — Fee page shows these change year to year (e.g., "3 partial scholarships awarded on a competitive basis" for one cohort); described qualitatively as limited/competitive rather than citing a specific count that may be intake-year-specific and could go stale.
