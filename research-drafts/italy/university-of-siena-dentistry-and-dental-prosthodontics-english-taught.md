# University of Siena — Dentistry and Dental Prosthodontics (English-taught)

Research date: 2026-07-07
Country: Italy | City: Siena
Status: Draft written and gate-checked; PUBLISH-READY (ok=true)

## Key facts (corroborated)

- **Institution**: University of Siena (Università degli Studi di Siena), a public Italian university.
- **Founded**: 1240, originally as the Studium Senese — one of the oldest continuously operating universities in Europe. Became a state institution in 1859. (Wikipedia infobox, confirmed via direct fetch of en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Siena)
- **Location**: Siena, Tuscany — a UNESCO World Heritage historic centre (listed since 1995), compact walled medieval city. ~15,000-16,000 students, roughly a third of the city's ~53,000 population.
- **Program**: Dentistry and Dental Prosthodontics, single-cycle, 6 years, taught in English — reported as the first fully English-taught dentistry degree in Italy, designed per ADEE (Association for Dental Education in Europe) recommendations and Italian Ministry of Education standards.
- **Curriculum shape**: Years 1-3 pre-clinical (anatomy, histology, pathology, pharmacology, dental materials, physiology, biochemistry); internships begin year 2; years 3+ add clinical/surgical subjects (gnathology, oral surgery, prosthodontics, implantology, periodontology); final year emphasizes direct patient treatment including digital dentistry.
- **Admission**: National IMAT entrance exam. English B2 proficiency required by end of year 1. Italian needed in practice for clinical/patient-facing training even though teaching is in English.
- **Seats**: Volatile and small for non-EU-resident applicants. Multiple 2025-cycle IMAT seat-tracking sources (AcadIMAT, "This Is Locomotive") report Siena dentistry's non-EU seats fell from ~12 to as few as 2 while EU seats rose from 23 to 33. Treated in the draft as a caveat, not a fixed number — must be reverified each cycle.
- **Tuition**: NOT asserted as a fixed figure. Siena is a public university using an ISEE (family-income-banded) fee system. Third-party sources describe a range (~EUR 156/year at the most subsidized band, up to ~EUR 5,000/year ceiling), but the official current fee table (apply.unisi.it/contents/content/8-tuition-fees-and-costs) was behind a Cloudflare/AWS WAF bot-challenge page and could not be fetched directly in this session. OMITTED from structuredFacts.programs numeric fee fields; captured only as an honest feeNotes range with a verify-directly instruction.
- **Accommodation**: No dedicated university halls for this program. Students use DSU Toscana (regional public student-welfare agency) residences (competitive, tied to the regional study grant/borsa di studio) or private rentals, in some cases via a university-partnered housing search service. Third-party figure of ~EUR 300/month for a shared room cited but not treated as authoritative.
- **Indian community/food**: Siena is small and does not have an established Indian community comparable to Milan or Rome. At least one Indian restaurant (Sindia Ristorante Indiano) operates in the city, and Indian grocery delivery (Dookan) serves the area. Larger options likely require travel to Florence (~1 hour away).
- **Safety**: Numbeo crime-index data and the university's own international-admissions blog both describe Siena as comparatively low-crime versus Rome/Florence/Milan; main risk is petty theft/pickpocketing in tourist-crowded areas, not violent crime.
- **Regulator framing (India)**: This is a DENTISTRY program, so the correct Indian regulator is the Dental Council of India (DCI), not NMC/FMGE. DCI generally requires a screening test + state dental council registration for foreign dental graduates (with limited exceptions). University-specific DCI recognition status for Siena's Dentistry and Dental Prosthodontics degree could NOT be corroborated from an official DCI source — presented as a verify-yourself caveat, not asserted recognition.

## What was OMITTED and why

1. **Exact current tuition fee** — omitted as a hard number. Reason: official fee page blocked by bot-verification wall (Cloudflare/AWS WAF); third-party figures conflict/are approximate; publishing a specific number risked being stale or wrong for a family-income-banded system. Kept only as a qualitative feeNotes range with an explicit instruction to verify with the university.
2. **Exact current non-EU seat count** — omitted as a fixed structured fact. Reason: multiple sources show the number is volatile year to year (12 → 2 reported drop for the 2025 cycle); presenting last year's number as current risks misleading applicants. Framed as a "things to consider" caveat instead.
3. **NMC/FMGE-style regulator claim** — deliberately NOT used. This is a dentistry program; NMC is the wrong regulator. Used DCI (Dental Council of India) screening-test framework instead, and did not assert recognition since no official DCI-specific statement about this university could be found.
4. **Specific hostel pricing as fact** — the EUR ~300/month figure is attributed to a third-party source (student housing aggregator) and not stated as an official/current price.
5. **WDOMS/dental-directory listing** — not asserted, since WDOMS is a *medical*-school directory; no dentistry-specific directory listing could be corroborated for this program, so no recognition-registry claim was made for this program.

## Method note

Direct `curl` fetches to unisi.it, en.unisi.it, dentistry.unisi.it, and apply.unisi.it all returned bot-verification challenge pages (Cloudflare "Just a moment..." / AWS WAF captcha) rather than page content, so official-site facts were corroborated via WebSearch result snippets (which reflect indexed/cached official-page content) cross-checked against multiple independent third-party study-abroad/IMAT-consultancy sources, Wikipedia (directly fetchable), and one official Indian-government regulator PDF (DCI). No fact was taken from a single source; every structured/narrative claim in the JSON draft is corroborated by at least two of the sources listed above.
