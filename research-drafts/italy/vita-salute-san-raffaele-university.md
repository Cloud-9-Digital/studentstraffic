## Key facts (corroborated)

- **Name**: Vita-Salute San Raffaele University (UniSR)
- **Type**: Private (not-for-profit)
- **Established**: 1996 (Department of Psychology first; Medical School opened 1998; Philosophy 2001) - confirmed via Wikipedia infobox and multiple secondary sources
- **City**: Milan, Italy - main campus co-located with San Raffaele Hospital and IRCCS research institute
- **Official website**: https://www.unisr.it/en
- **Affiliation**: San Raffaele Hospital / IRCCS scientific research centres (teaching hospital model)
- **Rankings mentioned on official site**: "1st in Europe" for Citations per Faculty, "1st in Italy" for Research Quality, "1 Faculty/Student in Italy" (QS World University Rankings 2027 / THE World University Rankings 2026, as cited on UniSR's own IMDP page)

## Program: International Medical Doctor Program (IMDP)

- Single-cycle Master's Degree in Medicine and Surgery, English-medium
- Duration: 6 years, 360 ECTS
- Admission: limited access via UniSR's own English-language entrance test (NOT the national IMAT) - 60 multiple-choice questions (36 logic/reading comprehension, 24 science subjects), 80 minutes
- AY2026-2027 quotas per official Call for Admission: 86 seats for EU candidates, 64 seats for non-EU candidates residing abroad
- Registration fee: EUR 200 (covers both test sessions)
- Language requirement: C1 CEFR English on enrollment; B1 Italian required by end of year 2 (compulsory Italian course provided years 1-2) because clinical rotations require patient interaction in Italian
- Curriculum: shared preclinical foundation for 5 semesters, then choice of 4 tracks from year 2 (General; Medicine and Surgery - early clinical exposure; Global Health; Translational Research), each (except General) capped at 20 students
- Clinical training at San Raffaele Hospital; mandatory licensing rotations before graduation grant an Italian medical license and access to postgraduate specialization
- Stated recognitions (per official program page): World Health Organization, UK General Medical Council (GMC), US ECFMG
- Final semester is class/exam-free, reserved for independent thesis research (can be done at UniSR or a partner institution)

## Accommodation

Two dedicated student residences:
- **Cascina Melghera**: 58 rooms, ~300m from main university building, single rooms with private bathroom
- **Cassinella**: 100 rooms, ~20 min walk/short drive from campus (Via Cassinella 38, Vimodrone)
- Both: furnished single rooms (desk, wardrobe, bookcase, safety box, mini-fridge), shared breakfast room, study rooms, gym, recreation room
- Allocated via annual application/call, full-academic-year commitment only
- No dedicated university accommodation office for off-campus housing; International Office provides contacts/guidance; students directed to YesMilano, Erasmus Student Network partners (Spotahome, Uniplaces) for private rentals
- Estimated overall living cost per official international-student page: EUR 1,000-1,200/month (room, food, transport, other expenses) - this is the university's own estimate, corroborated in direction (though not exact figure) by third-party cost-of-living guides citing EUR 1,100-1,800/month for Milan generally

## Indian student context (Milan)

- Milan has one of Italy's largest Indian/South Asian communities (estimated ~162,000 Indians in the broader Milan/Lombardy area per secondary sources - unofficial estimate, used only for general framing, not stated as precise in the published copy)
- Indian grocery stores: e.g., Krishna Indian Bazaar (Porta Venezia)
- Indian restaurants: Rangoli (est. 1997), Saravanaa Bhavan (South Indian), Cittamani (Ritu Dalmia), among others
- Informal tiffin/home-cooked meal services commonly arranged via student WhatsApp/Facebook groups

## Safety

- Milan generally considered one of the safer major Italian cities for students; violent crime rare
- Main realistic risk: petty theft/pickpocketing on crowded metro and in busy central tourist areas (Stazione Centrale, Duomo), especially after dark
- Neighborhoods flagged by expat/safety guides as higher-crime for housing purposes: Quarto Oggiaro, San Siro, Corvetto
- Framed in the draft as normal urban-safety precautions, not a high-risk warning
