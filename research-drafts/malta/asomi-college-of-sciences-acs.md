## THE central caveat — read this before publishing or advising students

ACS's Medicine and Dental Medicine degrees are **taught in Malta but awarded by the Medical University of Plovdiv, Bulgaria** (a WDOMS-listed medical school whose recognised base is Plovdiv, Bulgaria — confirmed via the WDOMS school record). ACS markets this explicitly as letting students "avoid relocating to Bulgaria."

India's National Medical Commission (NMC) Foreign Medical Graduate Licentiate (FMGL) 2021 framework, per independent secondary sourcing gathered during this research, generally expects:
- The entire course of study, clinical clerkship, and internship to be completed **at one foreign medical institution**, in **the country where that institution is actually located/WDOMS-listed**.
- **No part of training** to occur in a third country outside that institution's home jurisdiction.

ACS's Malta-delivery / Bulgaria-degree-award structure is exactly the kind of cross-border "twinning" arrangement that this rule appears designed to exclude. No official NMC statement specific to ACS, or to this particular ACS–Plovdiv partnership, could be found during this research pass (search engines and public web sources only — no NMC portal login/direct database access attempted).

**Decision made**: this draft does NOT assert NMC recognition, FMGE eligibility, or NExT eligibility for the Malta-delivered pathway. Every narrative field that discusses the programmes explicitly tells students to verify this in writing with NMC, ACS, and the Medical University of Plovdiv before enrolling. This is treated as the single most important honest caveat on the page — it is surfaced in the summary, both program notes, thingsToConsider, and two dedicated FAQ entries, rather than buried or omitted.

If this project's editorial bar requires a page to affirmatively state NMC/FMGE eligibility before publishing plans that lean on it, this specific point should be treated as **unresolved** and flagged to a human reviewer — the draft as written is honest but does not resolve the underlying regulatory question.
