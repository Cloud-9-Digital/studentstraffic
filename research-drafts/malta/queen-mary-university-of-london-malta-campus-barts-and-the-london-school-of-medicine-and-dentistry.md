## Key facts (corroborated)

- **Institution**: Queen Mary University of London's first international campus, delivering the MBBS curriculum of Barts and The London School of Medicine and Dentistry (QMUL's Faculty of Medicine and Dentistry) outside the UK.
- **Location**: Victoria (Rabat), Gozo -- Malta's second island, ~67 sq km, population under 40,000. Campus sits adjacent to Gozo General Hospital.
- **Type**: Public (Queen Mary University of London is a public UK Russell Group university; the Malta Campus is a branch of the same institution, not a separate private medical school).
- **Established**: Malta Campus building opened October 2019 (first MBBS Malta cohort graduated 2022 per Study Medicine Malta content).
- **Programme**: Medicine MBBS (Malta, 5-Year Programme). Same curriculum, assessments and problem-based learning structure as Queen Mary's London A100 MBBS. GMC-approved (UK regulator for medical education/training).
- **Intake size**: ~80 students/year (Malta track).
- **Entry requirements**: UCAT required (third decile and above; SJT Band 4 not considered), IELTS 7.0 overall (within 2 years) for non-native English speakers, minimum age 18 at course start. Apply directly to the university, not via UCAS. No application fee. No transfers permitted between Malta and London tracks, and no transfers accepted from students already enrolled in another medical course.
- **Fees**: EUR39,500/year for all students (UK, EU, international) for September 2026 entry, confirmed on the official programme FAQ. No separate international fee tier found. No scholarship/fee-waiver scheme confirmed in the sources reviewed -- **omitted** any claim of scholarships.
- **Teaching language**: English (one of Malta's two official languages; ~88% of Maltese speak English per Eurobarometer, cited on the official FAQ). Some patient-doctor interactions may occur in Maltese, translated/discussed in English with clinical tutors.
- **Clinical placements**: Year 1 -- GP placements + Gozo General Hospital + St Vincent de Paul (Malta). Years 3-5 -- Gozo General Hospital, Mater Dei Hospital, Karen Grech Hospital, St Vincent de Paul, plus other primary/secondary care sites.
- **Campus facilities**: Four-storey, ~8,100 sqm building -- clinical skills simulation centre, communication skills suite, lecture theatre, PC labs, seminar/PBL rooms, anatomy & physiology centre with dissection suite, library, student support services, rooftop dining/social areas.
- **Accommodation**: No halls of residence. Private rental only, mostly shared apartments in/near Victoria. University runs a pre-arrival WhatsApp group (with student union BSLA Malta) and a residency support contact (residency-malta@qmul.ac.uk) to help students find flatmates; recommended search window 3-6 months ahead of arrival.
- **Cost of living**: One official student account (third-year student, "What I spend in a day..." feature on the official Study Medicine Malta site) cites roughly GBP500-600/month total spend and a two-bedroom Victoria apartment at ~GBP430/month. This is presented as an illustrative, attributed personal account in the draft -- NOT an official cost-of-living schedule.
- **Student support**: Dedicated Student, Academic and Pastoral Support Office in Malta, linked to the wider Faculty of Medicine and Dentistry network in London; follows GMC pastoral-support guidance.
- **Safety**: Malta ranked 12th safest country globally in the World Justice Project's Rule of Law Index (per MaltaToday/angel-jobs.mt/lovinmalta.com coverage); national crime rate reported as declining over the past two decades. Presented with attribution, not as an unqualified "completely safe" claim.
- **Indian food/community**: Limited but present -- Cardamom (Indian-inspired restaurant, Victoria), Trishna's, and a vegan Indian food stall in Gharb. No dedicated Indian grocery store confirmed on Gozo; general international/African shop exists. Presented honestly as a limitation of island life, not a strength.

## Regulatory framing (important nuance)

This is a **UK-awarded, GMC-approved MBBS**, not a typical private non-UK "MBBS abroad" degree. That changes the correct India-audience framing:
- Do NOT assert blanket NMC/FMGE eligibility -- recognition of any foreign (including UK) medical degree in India is governed solely by India's National Medical Commission, and rules/eligibility criteria (minimum program duration, internship months, screening test regime, etc.) change periodically.
- The draft explicitly tells prospective Indian students to independently verify NMC recognition and FMGE/NExT requirements before applying, rather than asserting a status. This is the "honest caveat" the task specification calls for.

## What was OMITTED and why

- **Scholarships / fee waivers**: no official scholarship scheme was corroborated for the Malta MBBS track in the sources reviewed -- omitted entirely rather than fabricated.
- **Numeric hostel/accommodation fee**: QMUL Malta does not operate halls of residence, so there is no official per-year hostel fee to report. Only an attributed personal cost-of-living account is included, clearly marked as illustrative.
- **NMC/FMGE recognition status**: not asserted as confirmed either way -- flagged as something students must verify directly with the NMC, consistent with the "correct regulator only if corroborated" rule.
- **USD-converted tuition**: the EUR39,500/year figure is reported as-is (currency + amount + verification date) without an unsupported FX conversion, since no FX-rate source/date was corroborated for this draft.
- **"6-year" duration claim**: one low-quality aggregator (rmcedu.com via search snippet) described the programme as six years; this conflicts with QMUL's own official FAQ and course-finder listing, both of which consistently state 5 years. The official/first-party figure (5 years) was used, and the outlier was discarded as likely inaccurate rather than blended in.
- **qmul.ac.uk direct fetch**: automated curl fetches of qmul.ac.uk/malta pages returned a CloudFront 403 (blocked bot traffic) rather than page content. Facts attributed to "the official site" were corroborated via (a) Google/Bing search-result snippets of those exact qmul.ac.uk pages, and (b) QMUL's own Malta-programme recruitment microsite, studymedicinemalta.com (run by the same admissions/marketing team, contact smd-mbbs-malta@qmul.ac.uk), which fetched successfully via curl and is treated as a first-party/official-program source, cross-checked against independent press coverage.
