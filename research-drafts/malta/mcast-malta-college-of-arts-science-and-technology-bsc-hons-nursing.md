## Key institutional facts (corroborated)

- **MCAST** (Malta College of Arts, Science and Technology) is a **public** vocational and higher-education institution, **established in 2001**. [MCAST About page; Wikipedia]
- Headquartered at its **Paola Main Campus** (Triq Kordin, Paola PLA9032), with additional institutes/campuses in Mosta (Institute for the Creative Arts), Qormi/Luqa (Institute of Applied Sciences - Centre of Agriculture, Aquatics and Animal Sciences), and Gozo. Wikipedia describes it as seven institutes over four campuses; MCAST's own page describes six institutes plus the Gozo campus (minor discrepancy between the two independent sources, both corroborating the same multi-campus structure). [MCAST About page; Wikipedia]
- Roughly 6,500 full-time and 5,000 part-time students (Wikipedia infobox, sourced to MCAST). Offers 190+ full-time and 300+ part-time courses, MQF/EQF Level 1 to Level 8 (certificate to Doctoral). [MCAST About page]
- Holds **self-accrediting status** from Malta's National Commission for Higher and Further Education (MFHEA); degrees align with the European Qualifications Framework. [MCAST International Students page]
- Malta is bilingual (Maltese + English); MCAST programmes are delivered in English. [MCAST International Students page]

## Program 1: Bachelor of Science (Honours) in Nursing (AS6-W04-23) — courseSlug: bsc-nursing

- **MQF/EQF Level 6, 180 ECTS, 3 years full-time.** Administered by the Institute of Applied Sciences, Department of Applied Sciences. [MCAST official course page]
- Requires a minimum of **4,600 hours over 3 years** (2,300 theoretical + 2,300 clinical-practice hours), per **EU Directive 2005/36/EC**. Most practice hours are on clinical placements supervised by qualified nurse mentors. [MCAST official course page]
- Graduates who pass all theory/practice units can apply for registration with the **Nursing and Midwifery Council of Malta**. MCAST states the qualification "is recognised across all EU member states." [MCAST official course page]
- Admissions: values-based interview (+ Preliminary Interview stage for international applicants), occupational health-screening certificate, clean police conduct certificate. Entry requirements: specific MCAST Advanced Diplomas (Health Sciences / Health and Social Care / Social Care / Dental Surgery Assistants / Pharmacy Technicians) OR 2 A-Level + 2 I-Level passes; Maturity Clause route for age 23+. [MCAST official course page]
- **No tuition fee figure found** on MCAST's own official pages for EU or international/non-EU students during this research pass — correctly OMITTED from the draft rather than fabricated.

### Important caveat — Northumbria dual-award status
MCAST has run a **joint BSc (Hons) Nursing Studies dual-award** with UK partner **Northumbria University**, with shared teaching, Northumbria-registered nursing academic staff, and simulation facilities at MCAST. However, **Northumbria's own official course page for this specific joint route states "this course is no longer accepting new applications,"** with the most recent listed start date being October 2021. This draft treats MCAST's own standalone BSc (Hons) Nursing degree (still active in the 2026/27 prospectus) as the corroborated-current pathway, and explicitly flags that the UK dual-award route's current status must be verified directly with MCAST — it does NOT claim the Northumbria dual-award is currently open.

## Program 2: Undergraduate Certificate in Nursing Studies (Bridging Course) — courseSlug: bsc-nursing

- Reported (via WebSearch result summaries only — the live page at mg2imalta.com was **blocked by a Cloudflare bot-challenge** and could not be directly fetched) to be a short (~6 month) bridging program run via **MG2i**, described on MCAST's own About page as "MCAST's commercial arm."
- Intended for **non-EU-qualified nurses who already hold a BSc in Nursing** but need to cover specific missing learning outcomes required under EU Directive 2005/36/EC.
- Reported modules: Multidisciplinary Approaches in Health and Social Care, Quality Assurance in Health and Social Care, Ethical Issues and Decision Making, Work-Based Training in Health Science, Intermediate English, Gerontology, Mental Health.
- Reported outcome: eligibility to apply for registration with the Nursing and Midwifery Council of Malta.

## India-specific regulatory framing (Nursing -> INC)

- India's **Indian Nursing Council (INC)** recognizes foreign nursing qualifications on a **case-by-case, reciprocal-equivalence basis** via its Equivalence Committee (Section 11(2)(a), INC Act 1947) — NOT via a blanket pre-approved country/institution list.
- **No INC statement specific to MCAST or Malta-issued nursing qualifications was found.** The draft does NOT assert automatic INC recognition; it explicitly instructs students to apply to INC directly for equivalence assessment before relying on an MCAST degree for Indian registration purposes.
